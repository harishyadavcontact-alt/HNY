- But if we cannot express what something is exactly, we can say something about what it is not—the indirect rather than the direct expression.

- The “apophatic” focuses on what cannot be said directly in words, from the Greek _apophasis_ (saying no, or mentioning without mentioning).

- The method began as an avoidance of direct description, leading to a focus on negative description, what is called in Latin _via negativa_, the negative way, after theological traditions, particularly in the Eastern Orthodox Church.
