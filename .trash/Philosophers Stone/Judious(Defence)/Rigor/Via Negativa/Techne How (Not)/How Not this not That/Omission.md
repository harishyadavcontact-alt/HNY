- Acts of omission, _not_ doing something, are not considered acts and do not appear to be part of one’s mission.

- Yet in practice it is the negative that’s used by the pros, those selected by evolution: chess grandmasters usually win by not losing; people become rich by not going bust (particularly when others do); religions are mostly about interdicts; the learning of life is about what to avoid. You reduce most of your personal risks of accident thanks to a small number of measures.

    - Heuristics of Omission (Via Negativa)
	Via negativa focuses on subtraction (acts of omission) rather than addition (acts of commission), acknowledging that “the good is mostly in the absence of bad”.
- First, Do No Harm
	This principle (primum non nocere) means that interventions should be avoided if the benefits are small and risks are large. We should resort to medical techniques “only when the health payoff is very large (say, saving a life) and visibly exceeds its potential harm”.

- Burden of Evidence
	The burden of evidence falls on those who propose _via positiva_ policies or “disrupt the natural”. Nature is considered “much less of a sucker than humans”.
- Procrastination as Defense
	Procrastination, or delay, is “our natural defense, letting things take care of themselves and exercise their antifragility”. Fabius Maximus Cunctator (“the Procrastinator”) exemplifies this wisdom of voluntary omission.
- Simplicity and Removal
	Simpler solutions are preferred because “Complications lead to multiplicative chains of unanticipated effects”. Removing medication or unnatural stressors is often more robust than adding them.
