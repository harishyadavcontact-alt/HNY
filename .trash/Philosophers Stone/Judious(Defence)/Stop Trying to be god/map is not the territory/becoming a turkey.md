Fragile assumption of safety without foresight

---

## 🦃 What does “becoming a turkey” mean?

It’s a metaphor for **mistaking past stability for future safety**, especially when that safety is **based on limited data or flawed assumptions**.

---

### 📖 The Story (a Taleb classic):

> A turkey is fed every day by a kind human.
> 
> Day 1: food.
> 
> Day 50: more food.
> 
> Day 1000: still food.
> 
> The turkey grows more confident: “These humans love me!”
> 
> But on **Day 1001**, just before Thanksgiving…
> 
> 💀 _the butcher shows up._

---

### 🧠 What it _means_:

The turkey’s belief system was based on a **false sense of security**—drawn from past data ("I've always been fed = I always will be").

But what the turkey **didn’t see** was that its life was being _engineered toward a catastrophic event_ it couldn't imagine—because it lacked the perspective.

---

### 🚨 Becoming a Turkey = Fragility

- You become a turkey when you **trust the trend** without understanding **risk or randomness**.

- Especially when **what you don’t know** can kill you.

- Turkeys are **fooled by stability**, thinking things will continue linearly forever.

---

### 💣 Real-World Examples:

|   |   |
|---|---|
|🦃 "Turkey Thinking"|💥 Reality|
|“Housing prices always go up.”|2008 financial collapse|
|“This startup is growing every quarter.”|Sudden market crash, lawsuits, black swans|
|“This job is stable, I’ll always be employed here.”|Layoffs, automation, economic shifts|
|“This server has never crashed.”|But it will. At the worst possible moment.|

---

### 🧬 Turkey vs. Antifragile

- **Turkey**: Gets **weaker the longer things go well** (more dependent, less alert).

- **Antifragile system**: Gets **stronger** with shocks, randomness, and small failures.

---

### 🧘‍♂️ How to _not_ be a turkey:

- **Question smooth trends**—ask: what’s hiding behind this calm?

- **Look for hidden fragilities**.

- Embrace **optionality, redundancy, and skin in the game**.

- Prepare for **black swans**, don’t predict them.

---

### 💥 TL;DR

> “Becoming a turkey” means growing confident from repeated positive outcomes, blind to the fact that a massive blowup is being silently prepared behind the scenes.
