# Convergent Evolution

> When two unrelated lineages independently evolve the same trait because they face the same ecological pressures.

---

## Definition

Convergent evolution is not shared ancestry — it is independent discovery of the same solution to the same problem.

**Classic example:** The eye. Eyes evolved independently ~40 times in different lineages. The physics of photon detection constrain the solution space. The same tool gets invented over and over.

---

## Why It Matters

Convergent evolution is one of the strongest forms of evidence in biology:
- If it evolved independently multiple times, it *works* — it solves a real problem
- It is informative about **ecology** (microevolution), not ancestry (macroevolution)
- It reveals what the pressures of complex social life demand

---

## The Usual Suspects

The prime example in the Sophia Lectures: [[The Usual Suspects — Overview]].

Corvids, whales, dolphins, and primates all evolved:
- Advanced social complexity
- Long childhoods and plasticity
- Cultural transfer of information
- Theory of Mind (in some cases)

These lineages are not closely related. But they face similar ecological demands — complex social environments, long lives, generational learning. Convergent evolution shows these traits are *solutions*, not accidents.

---

## The Snake and Worm Problem

A snake and a worm both lack legs. Does their shared leglessness mean they are closely related? No — it means they both became fossorial (burrowing) or limbless for ecological reasons. Convergent loss, not shared ancestry.

This is why you need [[Consilience of Evidence]] — multiple independent lines — to infer true history.
