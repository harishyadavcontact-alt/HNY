# Descent with Modification

> Evolution's simplest, most foundational definition: all life shares common ancestry, modified over time by heritable change.

---

## The Core Idea

Darwin's central insight: species are not fixed. They change over time. New species are not created de novo — they descend from earlier species with modifications accumulated over generations.

Three processes:
1. **Heritable variation** — offspring differ from parents
2. **Selection** — some variants survive and reproduce better
3. **Deep time** — enough generations for small changes to accumulate into large differences

---

## Evolution Is Not a Ladder

A common misunderstanding: evolution is not progress toward a goal. There is no "higher" or "lower." It is a bush, not a ladder — diverging branches, each adapted to its own niche.

"Fitness" means fit for *a specific environment*, not abstractly superior.

---

## Connection to Science as Method

Science itself is a form of descent with modification: ideas that better predict reality survive and propagate; ideas that fail get selected against. This is why [[Consilience of Evidence]] matters — the fittest models are those that reconcile the most evidence with the fewest assumptions.
