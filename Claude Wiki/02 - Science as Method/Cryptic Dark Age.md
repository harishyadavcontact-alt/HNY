# Cryptic Dark Age

> We appear to be living in an age of scientific enlightenment. The pace of papers is at an all-time high. But the pace of *discoveries* is not. We have forgotten how science works.

---

## The Paradox

> "We are living in what I would call a cryptic dark age. Now that's going to sound paradoxical. It seems like we are living in an age of enlightenment. The pace of scientific work is probably at an all-time high. I feel almost certain of it. But the problem is that that pace of scientific work, the number of papers that are published, is not mirrored by the furious pace of profound new discoveries. In fact, if you know how to read the literature, you'll find a dearth of discoveries."
> — Bret Weinstein, Sophia Lecture 2

---

## The Evidence

Weinstein's claim: in evolutionary biology, there has not been a significant discovery since 1976 — when he was 7 years old. Not because there is nothing to discover. Because we have forgotten how to discover.

Symptom list:
- Scientists believe they have "evolved past" the scientific method
- Data collection is mistaken for science
- [[Consensus Is Not Science|Consensus]] has become the arbiter instead of [[Predictive Power — The Only Arbiter|predictive power]]
- Paradoxes are avoided rather than excavated
- Peer review has become a gatekeeping mechanism rather than a quality filter

---

## Loss of Ancient Wisdom

> "We've also lost the wisdom of the ancients. Maimonides was a philosopher who thought very deeply about medical questions and his wisdom is lost to modern medicine. We don't even think about it. It's too antiquated to bother ourselves. Yet, we're poisoning ourselves with all kinds of pharmaceuticals that he would have seen right through."
> — Bret Weinstein, Sophia Lecture 2

---

## How to Escape It

1. Return to the scientific method: observe, hypothesize, test, predict
2. Treat [[Paradox as Signal — X on the Map|paradoxes as signals]]
3. Reject [[Consensus Is Not Science|consensus]] as arbiter; use predictive power instead
4. Integrate ancient wisdom rather than dismissing it
5. Practice [[Scientific Humility]] — approach facts like a child
