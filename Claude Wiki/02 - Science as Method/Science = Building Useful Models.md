# Science = Building Useful Models

> Science is not a body of facts, a credential, or a consensus. It is a method for building models of reality that are *useful* — models that predict more, assume less, and fit together.

---

## The Definition

> "Science is the building of useful models. And it only works if you adhere to the underlying philosophy. Science is a method. Doesn't require you to have a degree. It doesn't require you to have a grant. Doesn't require you to be anything other than curious and ready to stick to the method."
> — Bret Weinstein, Sophia Lecture 2

Three criteria for a good scientific model:
1. **Predicts more** than the previous model
2. **Assumes less** (parsimony)
3. **Fits with other models** — consilience, no gaps

---

## Data Collection ≠ Science

The most common failure mode of modern "science":

**Observation alone is not science.** It is at best the first step. If you only collect data and never build or test models, you are not being scientific and should not expect great insights.

> "Data collection alone is not science. It doesn't work. At best data collection is the first step of the scientific method — observation. But if that's all you do, just repeated observation, you're not being scientific."
> — Bret Weinstein, Sophia Lecture 2

---

## The Method

```
1. Observation  (not science yet)
2. Hypothesis  (a model candidate)
3. Prediction  (what should be true if the model is right)
4. Test  (does reality match the prediction?)
5. Revision  (update the model)
→ Repeat
```

The arbiter is never credentials, peer review, or consensus. The arbiter is [[Predictive Power — The Only Arbiter|predictive power over the long term]].
