# Beringia — The Original Frontier

> A landmass four times the size of California that connected Alaska to Russia. The first Americans made their home here, then faced a choice: go back to a known world, go forward into an unknown one, or stay and drown.

---

## The Story

15,000+ years ago, a group of fully modern humans lived on Beringia — a vast grassland between Asia and North America. As the world warmed, sea levels rose, and Beringia began to disappear beneath the waves.

Three options:
1. **Go back west** — to Asia, a land known but occupied by others
2. **Go forward east** — into continents no human had ever seen
3. **Stay** — and be consumed by the rising sea

Nobody who survived chose option 3.

---

## What They Did

Some went east. They became the first Americans, populating two continents in total isolation from the Old World. Without any contact with Old World relatives, they independently:
- Invented written language
- Invented agriculture
- Developed hundreds of new ways of being human
- Reached a population of 50-100 million before European contact

**They did not have blueprints. They had ancestral knowledge, human ingenuity, and the [[The Campfire — Forge of Ideas|campfire]].**

---

## The Metaphor for Now

> "This story of Beringia, even if true only at the metaphorical level, is instructive of what it is to be human. It is an apt if incomplete metaphor for the situation that humanity is in today. We too find ourselves in a failing land. We too must seek new opportunities to save ourselves. And we too do not yet know what exploration will yield."
> — Heying & Weinstein

We are Beringians again. The known world is changing beneath our feet. The choice is the same: cling to the known and disappear, or explore the unknown and adapt.
