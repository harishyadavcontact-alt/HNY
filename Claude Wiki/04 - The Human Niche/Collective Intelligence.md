# Collective Intelligence

> The whole is greater than the sum of its parts. Not as cliché — as mechanism. When minds with non-overlapping blind spots and complementary skills converge, you get insights impossible for any individual.

---

## The Core Claim

> "It doesn't matter how smart an individual is, and it doesn't matter how much they know. In nearly every case, when minds come together, the whole is greater than the sum of its parts."
> — Heying & Weinstein

The resolution of [[The Human Paradox]] is not individual intelligence. No single human is a specialist in everything. The collective is.

---

## The Mechanism

Three required ingredients (from the Beringia campfire story):

**1. Complementary skills**
Bem (observer), Soo (fish watcher), Gol (engineer), Lok (rope-maker). Each knows something the others don't. Redundant skills are wasted; complementary skills multiply.

**2. Non-overlapping blind spots**
Each person can check the others' errors. Where you are blind, another sees. Where they are blind, you see. The collective has no blind spots — or far fewer.

**3. Political structure that allows novelty**
If the group automatically rejects unusual ideas, the mechanism fails. Innovation requires that minority-of-one ideas get a hearing. The campfire must be a safe space for weird hypotheses.

---

## Why Scale Matters

> "We need more than individuals processing in isolation. If we are to survive the future, we need multitudes of people plugging in and parallel processing. Joining minds in this way exponentially increases the ability of humans to solve problems."
> — Heying & Weinstein

Collective intelligence scales differently than individual intelligence. One person's intelligence grows slowly (learning, experience). A network of minds grows *multiplicatively* — each new member adds their unique perspective to a system that is already greater than the sum of its previous parts.

---

## Modern Threats

The conditions for collective intelligence are being eroded:
- Social isolation (we no longer know our neighbors)
- Echo chambers (people with identical blind spots aggregating together)
- Suppression of novelty (cancel culture = ending the third requirement)
- Digital polarization (driving people into ideological camps rather than complementary teams)
