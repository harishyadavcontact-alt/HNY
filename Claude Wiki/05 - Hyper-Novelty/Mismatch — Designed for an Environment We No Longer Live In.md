# Mismatch — Designed for an Environment We No Longer Live In

> The root cause of most modern disease, dysfunction, and misery: we are running Pleistocene software in a 21st-century environment.

---

## The Core Claim

> "The good news is you are exquisitely well-designed. The bad news is that you are exquisitely well-designed for an environment that we don't live in."
> — Bret Weinstein, Sophia Lecture 2

Every organ, drive, instinct, and preference we have was shaped by selection in specific ancestral environments — most recently, the hunter-gatherer EEA. Most of those environments no longer exist.

---

## The Rain in the Mountains

The book's opening story: Heying and Weinstein, living near a river in Costa Rica, are warned three times by a campesino: "Today there was rain in the mountains." They almost ignore him. The river nearly kills them.

> "At no other time in history has it been possible to think that you are a local but to be so lacking the deep knowledge of a place that keeps you safe during rare events."

We have been living by the river for a month. We think we know it. We don't.

**Remember to think about the rain in the mountains.** The danger is upstream, invisible, and real.

## Manifestations by Domain

Each of these is a mismatch problem, not a moral failing:

| Domain | Ancient Adaptation | Modern Context | Mismatch |
|---|---|---|---|
| Food | Seek calories, especially fat/sugar | Unlimited processed food | Obesity, metabolic disease |
| Sleep | Follow light cycles | Artificial light, shift work | Circadian disruption |
| Social | Small tribe, known faces | Anonymous urban millions | Loneliness epidemic |
| Sex | Complex evolved drives | Disconnected hookup culture | Relationship dysfunction |
| Parenting | Village raises children | Nuclear family isolation | Parenting stress, child outcomes |
| Medicine | Whole-body, wisdom-based | Symptom-suppressing, pharmaceutical | Chronic disease |
