# Food — Evolutionary View

> We eat like postindustrialists. Our guts are hunter-gatherers. This mismatch is one of the clearest sources of modern metabolic disease.

---

## The Evolutionary Context

For millions of years, humans ate:
- Whole, unprocessed foods
- Seasonal variety (no food available year-round)
- High dietary diversity (hundreds of plant species)
- Animal foods that were genuinely wild (different fatty acid profiles than farmed animals)
- No refined carbohydrates, no industrial seed oils, no artificial additives

Our digestive systems, microbiomes, metabolic pathways, and satiety signals all evolved in this context.

---

## The Mismatch

Modern industrial food:
- Ultra-processed, with palatability engineered to exceed any natural food signal
- Available 24/7, in unlimited quantity
- Nutritionally narrow (corn, wheat, soy in 80% of products)
- Chemically different from ancestral foods (grain-fed animals, industrial oils)

Our satiety signals, evolved in a world of food scarcity, did not anticipate unlimited palatability-optimized food. The "eat when you can" signal, which was adaptive in the EEA, becomes destructive when food is infinite.

---

## The Naturalistic Fallacy Warning

This analysis does not imply we must eat exactly as hunter-gatherers did — [[Naturalistic Fallacy|what is natural is not automatically what is optimal]] for modern people. But understanding the ancestral baseline clarifies which deviations matter most.

---

## Key Questions (Evolutionary Lens)

- Was this food available to our ancestors in anything like its current form?
- Does our gut microbiome have evolved enzymes/bacteria for this?
- Does this food produce the satiety signals it "should" produce?
- What signals is this food interfering with?
