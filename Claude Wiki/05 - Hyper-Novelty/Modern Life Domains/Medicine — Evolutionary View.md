# Medicine — Evolutionary View

> Modern medicine is extraordinarily good at emergencies. It is extraordinarily poor at chronic conditions — because it treats symptoms rather than root causes, and ignores the evolutionary context of disease.

---

## The Mismatch

**What we evolved with:** Environments where our bodies handled most challenges through evolved immune, metabolic, and healing systems. Healers had deep, accumulated wisdom about whole-body health.

**What we have now:** Pharmaceutical intervention targeting symptoms. Drugs that suppress signals (pain, fever, inflammation) rather than asking *why* the signal exists. A system that creates dependency rather than resilience.

---

## The Maimonides Problem

> "We've also lost the wisdom of the ancients. Maimonides was a philosopher who thought very deeply about medical questions and his wisdom is lost to modern medicine. We don't even think about it. It's too antiquated to bother ourselves. Yet, we're poisoning ourselves with all kinds of pharmaceuticals that he would have seen right through."
> — Bret Weinstein, Sophia Lecture 2

The [[Cryptic Dark Age]] is especially visible in medicine. We have traded depth of wisdom for breadth of data. We have more papers than ever and less understanding of health than our ancestors had.

---

## The Evolutionary Framework Applied

Every symptom is a signal. The evolutionary question is: what is this signal *for*?

- **Fever** — elevated body temperature makes it hostile for pathogens. Suppressing fever may extend illness.
- **Inflammation** — an evolved repair mechanism. Chronic suppression disrupts healing.
- **Pain** — a signal to stop doing damage. Masking it may allow continuation of the damaging behavior.
- **Depression** — possibly an adaptive withdrawal response. Medicating without understanding context may prevent the adaptation.

---

## The Question to Ask

For any medical intervention: is this treating the root cause or suppressing the signal? What would the evolutionary function of this symptom be? What environment was this response designed for?
