# Sleep — Evolutionary View

> Circadian rhythms evolved over billions of years. Artificial light has existed for 150. The mismatch is total.

---

## The Ancestral Pattern

For all of human evolutionary history:
- Sleep was governed by light — sunrise triggered waking, darkness triggered sleep
- Seasons altered sleep duration — longer nights meant longer sleep in winter
- No artificial light after dark — firelight has minimal circadian effect (warm spectrum)
- Sleep was not rigidly scheduled — polyphasic patterns (night sleep + afternoon rest) were common in many cultures

---

## The Mismatch

Modern industrialized sleep:
- Blue-spectrum artificial light suppresses melatonin for hours after sunset
- Fixed work schedules force wake times regardless of biological readiness
- All seasons look the same indoors — no seasonal circadian signal
- Average sleep duration has dropped ~1-2 hours in the past century
- Shift work forces sleep at biologically inappropriate times

---

## What Sleep Is For (Evolutionary Perspective)

Sleep is not passive rest — it is active, essential biological work:
- Memory consolidation and pruning
- Cellular repair and waste clearance (glymphatic system)
- Immune system regulation
- Hormonal reset (cortisol, growth hormone, insulin sensitivity)
- Emotional processing

All of these processes evolved to run during sleep, timed by circadian signals. When the signals are scrambled, the processes are scrambled.

---

## The Question

Not "how do I sleep better?" but "what is my sleep evolved for, and what signals does it require to work properly?"

Answer: darkness, cool temperatures, consistent light/dark cycles, and social safety (you don't sleep deeply when threatened).
