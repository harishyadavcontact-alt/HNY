# 4 Properties of Pattern Prevalence

> Four properties determine how common any pattern in the universe will be — from atoms to galaxies to organisms. This framework is selection before biology.

---

## The Four Properties

> "There are four properties that explain how common any pattern in the universe is."
> — Bret Weinstein, Sophia Lecture 2

| Property | Description | Example (Gold) | Example (Spiral Galaxy) |
|---|---|---|---|
| **1. Tendency to come into being** | How often does this pattern form? | Only created in supernovae — rare | Formed from rotating gas clouds |
| **2. Durability** | How long does it persist once formed? | Very durable — doesn't decay radioactively | Stable for billions of years |
| **3. Similarity** | How recognizable is it as a category? | Atomic number 79 — unambiguous | Characteristic spiral arm structure |
| **4. Tendency to accumulate limited stuff** | How good is it at capturing resources? | Gold accumulates protons efficiently | Gravity accumulates stars and planets |

---

## This Is Proto-Competition

The fourth property — tendency to accumulate limited stuff — is not true competition. Spiral galaxies don't *care* about accumulating stars. Gold doesn't want protons. But this looks structurally like competition, and it is a precursor to it.

> "This is a precursor, logically speaking, to what will go on to be competition. The universe has a precursor built into it and that precursor looks an awful lot like competition."
> — Bret Weinstein

---

## This Is Selection Before Biology

The four properties together = **selection**. Selection is the force that determines what patterns become common and what patterns become rare. This selection exists in physics and chemistry before any biology exists.

Add [[Heredity|heredity]] and you get [[Heredity + Selection = Adaptation|adaptation]]. But selection was already there.

---

## Why This Matters

It dissolves the apparent mystery of how competition and selection arose in biology. They didn't arise *in* biology — biology *inherited* them from the abiotic universe and added the ingredient (heredity) that transforms proto-competition into full adaptation.
