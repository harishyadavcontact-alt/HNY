# Cultural Transfer of Information

> The ability to pass learned knowledge — not just genes — across generations. The mechanism that extends evolution beyond biology.

---

## What It Is

Cultural transfer is the transmission of learned information (behaviors, techniques, knowledge) from one individual to another and across generations, without requiring genetic inheritance.

This is a form of [[Heredity|heredity]] — information passing — but operating at a different level. Cultural heredity can:
- Transfer within a generation (horizontal transmission)
- Transfer across generations (vertical transmission, like genetic heredity)
- Accumulate faster than genetic evolution
- Be modified rapidly without waiting for selection to act on genes

---

## Why It Is Universal in Sentient Life

Any organism complex enough to learn things that are not genetically pre-programmed needs a way to preserve that learning. If every generation starts from zero, the benefit of [[Long Childhood → Plasticity|plasticity]] is wasted — you spend years learning only to lose everything at death.

Cultural transfer solves this: the campfire effect. What one generation learns, the next inherits culturally. This is why [[The Campfire — Forge of Ideas|the campfire]] is so important — it is the mechanism of cultural transfer.

## In the Usual Suspects

| Species | Cultural Transfer Example |
|---|---|
| Humans | Language, written knowledge, institutions |
| Chimpanzees | Termite fishing with tools — taught by watching |
| Dolphins | Sponge use for bottom-feeding — specific maternal lineages |
| Whales | Regional song dialects passed across generations |
| Ravens | Problem-solving techniques spread through flock observation |

---

## Cultural vs Genetic Evolution

| Property | Genetic | Cultural |
|---|---|---|
| Speed | Generations | Years/months |
| Direction | Random mutation + selection | Intentional teaching + learning |
| Reversibility | Almost never | Relatively easy |
| Scope | All traits | Learned behaviors only |

Cultural evolution can outpace genetic evolution dramatically. This is both the mechanism of human success and the source of [[Hyper-Novelty]] — culture changes faster than biology can follow.
