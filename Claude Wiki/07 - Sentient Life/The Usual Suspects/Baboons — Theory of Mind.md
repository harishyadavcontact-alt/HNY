# Baboons — Theory of Mind

> Baboons — Old World monkeys — have demonstrated theory of mind. They know that what they believe may not be what another baboon believes.

---

## The Claim

> "These baboons have theory of mind. They have the ability to discern that what they think is true about something is not necessarily what some other baboon thinks is true about something."
> — Heather Heying, Sophia Lecture 3

This is not metaphor. It is a behavioral observation: baboons can model the epistemic states of other baboons. They know that another individual may have a *false belief* about the location of food, the identity of an intruder, or the intentions of a third party.

---

## Why This Matters

Theory of mind in baboons means:
- It predates the human lineage (baboons and humans diverged ~25 million years ago)
- It evolved in a complex, highly social environment — the savanna, in large troops
- It is a *solution to social complexity*, not a uniquely human trait

---

## Baboon Social Life

Baboons live in large, stratified troops (20-150 individuals) with:
- Complex dominance hierarchies that shift over time
- Long-term alliances and friendships
- Matrilineal descent determining much of rank
- Males migrating between troops

This level of social complexity requires sophisticated social modeling. You need to know who knows what, who is allied with whom, and how information asymmetries can be exploited. Theory of mind is not a luxury in this environment — it is a competitive necessity.

---

## The Convergence Pattern

Baboons, great apes, cetaceans, corvids, elephants — all with complex social environments, all developing theory of mind. The pattern is consistent with [[Convergent Evolution]]: social complexity selects for social cognition.
