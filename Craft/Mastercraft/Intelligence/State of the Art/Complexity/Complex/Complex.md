### What is an Ergodic Process? (Simple version first)  
A process is **ergodic** if:  
- The **average behavior of one single person (or system) over a very long time** = The **average behavior of many different people (or systems) at a single moment in time**.  
In plain English: If you follow **one individual for long enough**, you will see them display the full statistical distribution that you would see if you took a snapshot of 1,000 different individuals right now.  
### Non-ergodic → Most of real life (especially human performance and success)  
Almost everything important in life is **strongly non-ergodic**. This is the key insight that 99 % of people get wrong when they give or receive advice.  
#### Classic examples of non-ergodic systems  
| Domain | Time average of one person (over life) | Cross-sectional average (many people at one moment) | Ergodic? ||-------------------------|-----------------------------------------|------------------------------------------------------|----------|| Wealth / net worth | One person almost never goes from poor → billionaire → poor again many times | At any moment: a few billionaires, many broke people | NO || Becoming a bestselling author / famous actor / unicorn founder | Almost nobody gets 5–10 independent shots at it | At any moment you see a power-law: tiny % make 99 % of the money/fame | NO || Elite athletic performance (Olympic medals, NFL, etc.) | One athlete has essentially one peak window | Tiny % of the population wins almost all the medals | NO || Scientific impact (citations, Nobel prizes) | One scientist rarely has multiple independent careers | Power-law: a few people get almost all citations | NO || Getting shredded / staying <10 % body fat for decades | Most people who get there once stay there or drift only slowly | At any second, ~1–3 % of men are truly lean year-round | Almost NO |  
In non-ergodic processes, **who you are** (your identity, habits, strategy, environment) permanently alters your trajectory. Sampling different versions of yourself over time is impossible the way it is when flipping a coin 10,000 times.  
### Why this matters in real life – the practical consequences  
1. **You cannot validly use population averages to predict your own outcome** “On average, people who do X make more money” is almost meaningless advice in non-ergodic domains because the average is dominated by the extreme winners. You are not going to become the average — you are going to become either one of the failures or one of the rare winners. There is almost no middle.  
2. **You have to aim for the extreme strategy, not the average one** In ergodic games (lottery, roulette, most exams), the optimal strategy is the same as the average strategy. In non-ergodic games (startups, artistic careers, elite physique, extreme wealth), the only strategies that survive are the ones that can absorb huge variance and occasionally hit the far tail. Half-measures get crushed.  
3. **Identity and irreversible choices trump effort in the long run** Because you don’t get to sample the distribution many times, the fastest way to change your personal statistics is to **change who you are permanently** (environment, peer group, daily systems, self-image). Example: Moving to Miami / Dubai / Silicon Valley for 10 years changes your wealth trajectory far more than reading 5 more finance books while staying in the same small town — because wealth is non-ergodic.  
4. **“Optional” things become mandatory if you want the tail outcome** Sleep 9 hours, lift 6×/week, write every morning, never drink, live near the best people in your field — these look “extreme” or “optional” from the population average, but they are table stakes for the people who actually end up in the top 0.01 % — because the process is non-ergodic.  
### How to apply ergodicity in real life – concrete rules  
| Goal (non-ergodic) | Ergodic thinking (wrong) | Non-ergodic thinking (correct) ||---------------------------------|-------------------------------------------------------|------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------|| Get rich | “On average, side hustles make $500/month” | Target the 1-in-10,000 outcome (e.g., build for acquisition or public company) or don’t play || Get world-class lean / muscular | “Most people who diet get to 15 % body fat” | Copy exactly what the top 0.1 % do (drugs + training + food + sleep) because 12 % is common there, rare elsewhere || Become a famous creator | “Post 3× week like the average successful account” | Post 3–10× per day, optimize for virality, move to the hub city — because only the extreme wins || Maximize health & longevity | “Population average life expectancy is 78” | Do everything the top 1 % of 95-year-olds do (zone 2 cardio, sauna, perfect sleep, no seed oils, etc.) |  
### The one-sentence rule to live by  
**In non-ergodic games (almost everything that matters), study and copy the daily life of the people who already have what you want — do not average advice from the 99 % who didn’t make it.**  
That is how you hack a non-ergodic world: you deliberately put yourself on the trajectory that only 1 in 10,000 people occupy, because averaging with the other 9,999 is a mathematical path to mediocrity or failure.
