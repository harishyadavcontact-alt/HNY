# Abiotic

> Relating to the non-living physical world. Rocks, weather, oceans, stars — all abiotic. Pattern change in the abiotic universe follows physical laws alone. There is no striving, no heredity, no adaptation.

---

## Definition

**Abiotic** = not biotic; not living. The abiotic universe is the physical world that exists independent of life:
- Elements and compounds (hydrogen, gold, water)
- Physical forces (gravity, electromagnetism)
- Geological and astronomical processes (weather, shoreline change, stellar evolution)

Abiotic patterns change over time. The sun changes. Shorelines erode. Mountains rise and fall. But this is not evolution — it is physics.

---

## Abiotic Selection

Selection — the differential persistence of patterns based on their properties — exists in the abiotic universe before any biology appears. The four properties that determine how common any pattern will be:

1. **Tendency to come into being** — how often does this pattern arise?
2. **Durability** — how long does it persist once it exists?
3. **Similarity** — is it recognizable as a category?
4. **Tendency to accumulate limited stuff** — how good is it at capturing resources?

These four properties determine how common gold is, how common spiral galaxies are, how common any physical pattern is. The fourth property — accumulating limited stuff — has a proto-competitive flavor, even in the abiotic world.

> "Selection is not the central element of biology. Selection exists in the universe before there's any biology."
> — Bret Weinstein, Sophia Lecture 2

---

## Abiotic vs Biotic Change

| | Abiotic | Biotic |
|---|---|---|
| Driver | Physical laws | Physical laws + heredity + selection |
| Direction | Non-directional | Directed toward fitness |
| Memory | None | Encoded in genome |
| Accumulation | Does not accumulate adaptations | Accumulates adaptations |
| Striving | Does not strive | All organisms strive |

Water flows downhill. A salmon swims uphill. Both are mostly water. The difference is heredity.

---

## Why the Distinction Matters

Mistaking an abiotic process for a biotic one — or vice versa — leads to bad explanations. Evolution (descent with modification) applies only to biotic entities. Rocks don't evolve. Shorelines don't evolve.

> "Those things that are evolutionary are biotic. They are life. And that includes trees and forests, coral and coral reefs, sheep and dragonflies and mushrooms and hummingbirds and horses and humans."
> — Heather Heying, Sophia Lecture 1
