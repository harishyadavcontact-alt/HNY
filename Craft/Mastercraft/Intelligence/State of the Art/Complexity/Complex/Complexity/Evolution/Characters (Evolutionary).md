# Characters (Evolutionary)

> The technical term for the individual traits that change across generations — the modifications in "descent with modification." Not to be confused with "characteristics" in the general sense. Characters are the building blocks of macroevolutionary inference.

---

## The Definition

A **character** is an individual heritable trait that can change across lineages over evolutionary time. Characters are what macroevolutionary biologists examine when they try to reconstruct who is related to whom.

> "Characters are the individual things that change over time — the modifications that happen in that 'descent with modification.' What are the characters that you can look at and say, 'That thing changed'?"
> — Heather Heying, Sophia Lecture 1

Every organism is a bundle of characters. Comparing which characters different lineages share — especially derived characters (new ones) vs ancestral ones — allows biologists to reconstruct the tree of life.

---

## Why "Characters" Not "Characteristics"

The use of "characters" rather than "characteristics" is a technical distinction the field insists on. It forces precision: you are not describing a general feature (a "characteristic") but a specific, potentially heritable, potentially variable unit (a "character") that can be tracked across lineages.

A characteristic is a description. A character is a unit of evolutionary analysis.

---

## Willi Hennig's Contribution

Before the German entomologist **Willi Hennig** (active mid-20th century), evolutionary biologists were often working from gestalt impressions — organisms that looked similar were assumed to be closely related.

Hennig formalized the requirement to identify specific characters and distinguish between:
- **Ancestral characters** (shared because they were present in a common ancestor)
- **Derived characters** (shared because they evolved recently, in the lineage you're studying)

Only **shared derived characters** are evidence of close relationship. Shared ancestral characters just mean you share a more distant ancestor.

This distinction is why the gestalt fails:
- Seals and whales both live in water (ancestral characters of their respective lineages, not evidence of shared recent ancestry)
- Bears and seals share specific derived molecular and morphological characters (genuine close relationship)

---

## The Four Types of Characters

| Type | Description |
|---|---|
| **Molecular** | DNA sequence data — the most data-rich, most recently available, but not sufficient alone |
| **Morphological** | Physical structures — skulls, teeth, limbs, wings, gills; can get fractal (measure parts of brains, margins within brains) |
| **Developmental** | Ontogenetic patterns — embryological stages, timing, developmental sequence |
| **Behavioral** | Behavioral traits — most variable and most noisy, but most interesting for microevolution; useful for macroevolution with caution |

A fifth type — **symbolic** (language evolution, phylogenies of language families) — is rare but valid. Languages evolve and can be analyzed like organisms.

---

## The Critical Principle: Consilience

No single character type is sufficient to reconstruct history reliably. You need consilience — multiple independent lines of evidence converging on the same conclusion.

A molecular phylogeny plus morphological characters plus developmental characters that all agree on the same relationship: strong evidence.

A molecular phylogeny that says one thing, morphology that says another: contradiction to be resolved, not one to be dismissed.

> "You can't have consilience between different kinds of evidence if you only have one kind of evidence."
> — Heather Heying

---

## Characters vs the Gestalt

The history of macroevolutionary biology is partly a history of the gestalt being wrong:

- Crocodiles look more like lizards than birds → false: birds and crocodiles are closest living relatives
- Seals look more like whales than bears → false: seals and bears are closest living relatives
- Whales must be far from anything we know → false: whales' closest extant relatives are hippos

Drill down to specific characters. The gestalt misleads.
