# Culture vs Consciousness

> The two modes humans oscillate between. Culture is received wisdom efficiently transmitted. Consciousness is newly packaged cognition available for exchange. The human niche is the oscillation between them.

---

## Definitions

> "Culture we define as beliefs and practices that are shared and passed between members of a population. These beliefs are often literally false, metaphorically true — they result in increased fitness if one acts as if they are true, despite the fact that they are either inaccurate or unfalsifiable."
> — Heying & Weinstein

> "Consciousness we define as that portion of cognition that is newly packaged for exchange — meaning that conscious thoughts are ones that could be delivered if someone asked what you were thinking about."
> — Heying & Weinstein

| Culture | Consciousness |
|---|---|
| Received from ancestors | Generated in the moment |
| Low mutation rate | High mutation rate |
| Efficient execution | Exploratory innovation |
| For stable environments | For novel environments |
| The zone | Problem-solving space |
| Sacred | Shamanistic |
| Conservative | Progressive |

---

## The Oscillation

Homo sapiens oscillate between two dominant modes:

**When times are good** (familiar environment, culture works): stay the course. Apply ancestral wisdom. Be comparatively conservative — protecting what works.

**When things aren't going well** (novel environment, culture fails): shift to consciousness. Pool minds. Parallel process. Be comparatively progressive — willing to endure the risks of change.

> "The human niche is niche switching — the ability to swap out our software and replace it as the need arises by oscillating between culture and consciousness."
> — Heying & Weinstein

---

## The Nez Perce Example

The Nez Perce people of the Pacific Northwest learned to distinguish camas (nutritious bulbs) from death camas (toxic) — virtually indistinguishable when not in flower. The first arrivals learned through painful trial and error (consciousness mode). By the 19th century, the distinction was nearly perfect — encoded in cultural practice.

When humans are exploiting a well-understood opportunity: **culture reigns**.
When novelty renders ancestral wisdom inadequate: **we need to shift to consciousness**.

---

## The Problem Today

> "Novel levels of novelty, such as we are experiencing now, are a special danger. This means that what's needed today — and urgently — is a call to consciousness on a scale that we have not seen before."
> — Heying & Weinstein

The pace of change ([[Hyper-Novelty]]) means culture is perpetually out of date. But we keep defaulting to culture (established patterns) because that is what stability-adapted organisms do. The mismatch is dangerous.
