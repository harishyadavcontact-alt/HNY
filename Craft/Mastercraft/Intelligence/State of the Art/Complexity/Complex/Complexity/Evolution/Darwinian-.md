# Darwinian

> Relating to Charles Darwin and the mechanism of evolution he identified: descent with modification through natural and sexual selection. Darwinian evolution is the bedrock framework for understanding all of biology and, by extension, all of human behavior.

---

## Darwin's Core Contribution

Charles Darwin (1809–1882) identified the mechanism of evolutionary change: **descent with modification** through **natural selection**.

The insight:
1. Organisms vary in heritable traits
2. Some variants survive and reproduce more than others
3. Therefore, over time, populations change — accumulating adaptations

Darwin's humility was also notable: his first sketch of an evolutionary tree bore the words "I think" — a recognition that even the most profound insight is a hypothesis to be tested.

---

## Natural and Sexual Selection

Darwin identified two forms of selection:
- **Natural selection**: differential survival and reproduction based on ecological fit
- **Sexual selection**: differential mating success based on mate preferences and competition — the driver of elaborate displays, weapons, and behavioral repertoires

Both operate through the same mechanism: heritable variation + differential reproductive success.

---

## The Modern Synthesis

Darwin didn't know about genes. The integration of Mendelian genetics with Darwinian selection (the Modern Synthesis, mid-20th century) filled this gap. The Selfish Gene (Dawkins, 1976) was the last major synthesis in the field — codifying the gene-centered view of selection and opening the door to understanding cultural evolution.
