# Dominance Hierarchy

> A rank-ordered social structure in which individuals occupy positions that determine their access to resources, mates, and status. Dominance hierarchies appear across social species — they are evolutionary solutions to the problem of managing intra-group competition.

---

## Definition

A dominance hierarchy is a stable rank-ordering of individuals within a social group, in which higher-ranked individuals generally have priority access to resources, mates, and preferred positions.

Dominance hierarchies are not about cruelty — they are evolved systems for converting potentially destructive competition into predictable, manageable outcomes. A hierarchy with clear ranks reduces constant fighting by making the outcome of conflicts predictable before they happen.

---

## Universal in Social Species

Dominance hierarchies appear across social animals:
- **Baboons**: separate male and female hierarchies, each with their own rules; every individual knows everyone else's rank in both hierarchies
- **Wolves**: pack hierarchies determine breeding rights and feeding order
- **Primates**: well-documented hierarchies in chimpanzees, gorillas, bonobos
- **Humans**: hierarchies organized by kinship, age, sex, skill, wealth, and status markers

> "We have status, governed by rules stemming from kinship, age, sex, and beyond. We have rules of succession and markers of hierarchy."
> — Heying & Weinstein (Human Universals)

---

## Why Hierarchies Are a Universal

Hierarchy is listed among the [[Craft/Mastercraft/Intelligence/State of the Art/Complexity/Complex/Complexity/Evolution/Human Universals]] because it is not a cultural choice — it is an evolved solution to the problem of managing intra-group competition in social species. Groups without hierarchy spend too much energy on conflict resolution. Groups with hierarchy can channel competition into structured, lower-cost contests.

---

## Plan on Taking Your Place

Every individual in a social species must eventually locate their position in the hierarchy. This is not optional — it is one of the key tasks of development and social integration.
