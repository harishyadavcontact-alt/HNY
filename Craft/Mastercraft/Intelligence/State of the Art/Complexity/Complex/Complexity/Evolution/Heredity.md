# Heredity

> Information transfer across generations. Combines with [[Craft/Mastercraft/Intelligence/State of the Art/Complexity/Complex/Complexity/Evolution/Selection]] to produce [[Adaptation]] — the defining threshold between the abiotic and biotic universes.

---

## What It Is

Heredity = the passing of information from parent to offspring. Not just DNA — anything that reliably transmits information across generations:

| Type | Mechanism | Speed |
|---|---|---|
| Genetic | DNA from parent to offspring | Generational |
| Epigenetic | Gene expression patterns passed down | Generational |
| Cultural | Learned behaviors, knowledge, practices | Within/across generations |

---

## Why It Is the Critical Ingredient

Selection exists in the abiotic universe. But without heredity, nothing improves — selection merely filters existing patterns without accumulating adaptive information.

**The equation:** [[Craft/Mastercraft/Intelligence/State of the Art/Complexity/Complex/Complexity/Evolution/Selection]] + Heredity = [[Adaptation]]

Once you add heredity, organisms can build on previous generations. Complexity compounds. Traits that work get passed forward and refined. This is what makes life special — not selection (which was already there) but heredity added to selection.

---

## Genetic Heredity

The primary mechanism: DNA carries genetic information from parents to offspring. Variation arises through mutation, recombination, and other mechanisms. Selection filters which variants survive to reproduce.

You are a 30 trillion cell organism with ~20,000 active genes. Most genes are **silent** in any given cell type — the digestive enzyme genes are silent in the eye. Genes have what Weinstein calls "unbridled ambition and zero vanity" — they will be silent for generations if that is what gets them into the future.

---

## Cultural Heredity

Cultural transmission extends heredity beyond biology. Information that would otherwise be lost at death can be passed to the next generation through teaching, story, ritual, and institution.

This is the mechanism of [[Craft/Mastercraft/Intelligence/State of the Art/Complexity/Complex/Complexity/Evolution/Campfire|the campfire]] — what one generation learns, the next inherits. It is why cultural evolution is so much faster than genetic evolution.

> "Culture we define as beliefs and practices that are shared and passed between members of a population. Culture is received wisdom, generally handed to you by ancestors, and efficiently transmitted."
> — Heying & Weinstein
