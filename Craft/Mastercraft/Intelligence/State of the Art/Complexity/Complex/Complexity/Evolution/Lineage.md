# Lineage

> A lineage is an individual and all of that individual's descendants. Fractally nested — you belong to many lineages simultaneously. Selection works across all levels at once.

---

## Definition

> “A lineage is an individual and all of that individual's descendants. So, if you don't have any kids, you're a lineage. If you have kids and grandkids, then you and your kids and grandkids are a lineage.”
> — Bret Weinstein, Sophia Lecture 2

An individual and all its descendants comprise a lineage. A species is a lineage descended from that species' most recent common ancestor — as too are larger clades (mammals, vertebrates, all animals).

---

## Fractal Nesting

You exist in nested fractal lineages simultaneously:
- You alone (lineage of one)
- Your immediate family
- Your extended family
- Your community
- Your nation (functions as a lineage)
- The entire human lineage
- Primate lineage → mammalian lineage → all life

> “Adaptive evolution is therefore fractal, and the term that encapsulates it is lineage. Our job as evolutionary biologists is to figure out how adaptive evolution works with selection at all levels of lineage occurring simultaneously.”
> — Heying & Weinstein

Selection is constantly figuring out which level of lineage to prioritize at any given moment.

---

## Why Lineage Resolves the Fitness Problem

**The Notre Dame problem:** Maurice de Sully (celibate Bishop of Paris, 1160) initiated construction of Notre Dame — took 600 years to complete. Zero personal reproductive success. But as member of many lineages that benefited, his contribution was evolutionarily sensible. Not a pathology (as Dawkins would call religion). A product of lineage-level selection.

**The eusociality problem:** Worker ants forego all reproduction to serve the queen. Termites do the same — *without* the haplodiploidy genetic system that explains ants. Naked mole rats too. The kin selection shortcut doesn't explain all of them. Lineage does.

> “Once we start thinking in terms of lineage and we stop thinking in terms of individual reproductive success, we can begin to get an indication of how this might be evolutionarily sensible.”

---

## The Illusion of Self

Our obsessive self-focus is not because selection cares especially about us individually. It is because evolution occurred when our reach was small — so self-focus was a reasonable proxy for lineage-focus. That proxy no longer maps well in a world of billions.

---

## CONSILIENCE: The Key Principle
- The strongest scientific inferences come from consilience — the convergence of independent lines of evidence
- Cannot have consilience with only one kind of evidence
- Not just molecular, not just morphological — must draw from multiple types
