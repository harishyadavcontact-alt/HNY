# Pattern Change

> Change over time is universal — it occurs in both the abiotic and biotic worlds. But only in the biotic world does change accumulate directionally toward fitness. The difference between a wave and an evolving lineage is heredity.

---

## Abiotic Pattern Change

In the abiotic world, patterns change continuously:
- Shorelines erode and reform
- Weather cycles through seasons
- Stars are born and die
- Mountains rise and fall

These changes follow physical laws. They are not random, but they are not *directed* in any biological sense. No abiotic pattern "tries" to persist. No abiotic change accumulates adaptive information.

---

## Biotic Pattern Change

In the biotic world, pattern change is different in kind. Living organisms change over time through:
- **Mutation** — changes in the information substrate (DNA)
- **Selection** — differential persistence of variants
- **Heredity** — transmission of information to descendants

This combination produces directional, cumulative change: **evolution**. The pattern doesn't just change — it accumulates adaptations. Each generation inherits the selected variants of the last.

> "As soon as you put selection and heredity together, you get adaptation."
> — Bret Weinstein

---

## The Key Difference

| Abiotic Change | Biotic Change |
|---|---|
| Physical laws only | Physical laws + heredity + selection |
| Non-directional | Directional (toward fitness) |
| Does not accumulate | Accumulates adaptations |
| Patterns recur | Patterns build on each other |
| No memory | Memory encoded in genome |

A hurricane and a whale both change over time. But the whale's lineage carries forward what worked. The hurricane does not.

---

## Microevolution vs Macroevolution

Pattern change in the biotic world operates at two timescales:
- **Microevolution**: change within a lineage over observable timescales — small changes in frequency of traits
- **Macroevolution**: the large-scale patterns — origins of new body plans, major transitions, the history of life
