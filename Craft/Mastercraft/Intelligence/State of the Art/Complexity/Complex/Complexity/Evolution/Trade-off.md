## Trade-Offs: The Inescapable Constraints

Trade-offs are the currency of evolution. Every adaptation comes at a cost. Energy spent on a large brain cannot be spent on powerful muscles. Time spent learning cannot be spent reproducing. Resources allocated to survival today reduce resources available for future reproduction.

Understanding trade-offs is essential to understanding why organisms are the way they are. Evolution does not optimize for perfection—it optimizes for **fitness within constraints**.

- **Trade-offs:** The fundamental biological and economic rule that you cannot maximize every trait or value at the same time. For example, _allocation trade-offs_ dictate that using finite resources for one thing means taking them from another (e.g., growing larger antlers costs bone density elsewhere). _Design constraint trade-offs_ dictate that you cannot physically be both the fastest and the most maneuverable.
