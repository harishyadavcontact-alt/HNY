# Vertical Evolution

> The standard mode of evolution — descent through generations. Parent to offspring. Information passed vertically down a lineage. Contrasted with horizontal gene transfer (bacteria) or cultural horizontal transmission.

---

## Definition

**Vertical evolution** is the transmission of genetic information from parent to offspring — the standard mechanism of biological inheritance and evolution.

Information flows:
- **Vertically**: from parent generation to offspring generation (down the lineage)
- Across reproductive events
- With fidelity, but with small modifications (mutations) at each step

This is "descent with modification" — the mechanism Darwin identified.

---

## Contrast: Horizontal Gene Transfer

In bacteria and some other microorganisms, genes can move **horizontally** between unrelated organisms in the same generation. This dramatically accelerates the spread of adaptive traits (like antibiotic resistance) across populations.

Vertical evolution is slower — it requires reproductive events and generational turnover. But it accumulates adaptations with greater precision and coherence across a lineage.

---

## Vertical vs Cultural Transmission

Cultural information can be transmitted both vertically (parent to child) and horizontally (peer to peer, across lineages). This is what makes cultural evolution so fast and flexible compared to genetic evolution.

- **Vertical genetic transmission**: one genome → one offspring, with small modifications
- **Vertical cultural transmission**: parent's knowledge → child's knowledge (the standard mode in human societies)
- **Horizontal cultural transmission**: peer-to-peer, innovator to population (can spread across unrelated lineages in one generation)
