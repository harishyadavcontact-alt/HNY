# WEIRD

> **W**estern **E**ducated **I**ndustrialized **R**ich **D**emocratic. The acronym for the narrow slice of humanity that most psychological, sociological, and medical research is based on — and then generalized to all humans. A profound sampling error.

---

## What It Means

WEIRD societies represent a tiny fraction of human history and human variation. Most people who have ever lived were not WEIRD. Most hunter-gatherer, agricultural, and traditional societies are not WEIRD.

Yet the majority of research in psychology, medicine, nutrition, and social science is conducted on WEIRD populations — often undergraduate college students in Western universities — and then claimed to reveal universal human nature.

---

## Why It Matters for the Evolutionary Framework

The WEIRD world is also the world of [[Craft/Mastercraft/Intelligence/State of the Art/Complexity/Complex/Complexity/Evolution/Chapter 3- Ancient Bodies, Modern World|Hyper-Novelty]]. It is the environment most radically diverged from the ancestral environments in which our biology was shaped.

Studying WEIRD people to understand human nature is like studying a bee trapped in a house to understand bee behavior. The sample is systematically biased toward a novel, mismatched context.

> "Most people in the WEIRD world in the West have been traumatized by what often passes for science. It's used as this weapon of blunt force."
> — Heather Heying, Sophia Lecture 1

---

## Implications

1. **Medical research:** Drug trials, nutritional studies, and psychological findings based on WEIRD populations may not generalize to the rest of humanity — or to ancestral human conditions
2. **Psychological universals:** Many "universal" psychological findings have failed to replicate in non-WEIRD populations
3. **The naturalistic fallacy risk:** What is "normal" in WEIRD societies is often radically abnormal in evolutionary terms

---

## The Corrective

The evolutionary framework provides a WEIRD-independent baseline — understanding what humans were adapted for (EEA) gives you a reference point that doesn't require any particular cultural context.
