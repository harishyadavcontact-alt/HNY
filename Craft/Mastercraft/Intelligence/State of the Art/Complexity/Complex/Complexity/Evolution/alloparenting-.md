# Alloparenting

> Caregiving behavior — like that of a parent — provided by an adult toward an individual who is not their direct offspring. Alloparenting is a key mechanism of human cooperative child-rearing and cultural transmission.

---

## Definition

**Alloparenting**: parental-style care provided by an individual toward offspring that are not their own. The "allo-" prefix means "other" — care from someone other than the biological parent.

Forms of alloparenting:
- **Grandparenting** — particularly grandmothers in human societies
- **Sibling caregiving** — older siblings helping raise younger ones
- **Aunting/Uncling** — extended family care
- **Group caregiving** — community-level child-rearing

---

## In the Human Context

Humans are exceptionally alloparental among primates. Human children require far more care and for far longer than any other primate — our long childhoods and profound helplessness at birth make solo parenting extraordinarily difficult.

The **Grandmother Hypothesis** proposes that menopause evolved partly to free post-reproductive females to invest in grandchildren — a form of alloparenting that substantially increases grandchildren's survival and flourishing.

---

## Cultural Transmission

Alloparenting is not just about survival — it is about cultural transmission. When adults other than biological parents invest in children, they transmit cultural knowledge, values, and skills. This is how civilizational knowledge accumulates beyond what any two parents could provide.

> "Adults are expected to help socialize children. Children observe elders, and copy them."
> — Heying & Weinstein (Human Universals)
