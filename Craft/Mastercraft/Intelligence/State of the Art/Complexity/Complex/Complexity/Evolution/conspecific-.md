# Conspecific

> A member of the same species. Competition between conspecifics is often the most intense form of competition — because they occupy the same ecological niche and seek the same resources.

---

## Definition

**Conspecific**: belonging to the same species. Conspecifics share the same gene pool, the same basic resource requirements, and the same reproductive arena.

---

## Why Conspecific Competition Is Intense

Since the earliest life, the competition that matters most has often been between conspecifics — organisms competing for the same mates, food sources, territory, and status within a shared social system.

In social species, conspecifics are both your greatest competitors and your most essential allies. This tension shapes the evolution of:
- **Dominance hierarchies** — managing intra-group competition
- **Reciprocal altruism** — cooperating with conspecifics for mutual benefit
- **Coalition-building** — allying with some conspecifics against others

---

## In the Evolutionary Framework

From the ecological dominance of humans over other species, competition between conspecifics has been the dominant selective pressure for millions of years. This has refined our capacities for social intelligence, deception, cooperation, and status competition to an extraordinary degree.
