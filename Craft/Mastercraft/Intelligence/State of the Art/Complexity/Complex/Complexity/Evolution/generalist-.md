# Generalist

> A species or individual with broad tolerances, adapted to a wide range of conditions and resource types. Generalists can survive in more environments but typically exploit each less efficiently than a specialist would. Humans are extreme generalists at the species level, with highly specialized individuals.

---

## Definition

A **generalist** is an organism adapted to a wide range of ecological conditions — tolerating many food types, habitats, temperatures, or social situations. Generalists sacrifice peak efficiency in any one niche for broad survivability across many niches.

Compare with **[[Craft/Mastercraft/Intelligence/State of the Art/Complexity/Complex/Complexity/Evolution/specialist-]]**, which is adapted to a narrow niche and excels within it but cannot survive if conditions change.

---

## Humans as Extreme Generalists

> "We are a broadly generalist species, with highly specialized individuals, who have shape-shifted and niche-shifted into nearly every environment on the planet."
> — Heying & Weinstein

Humans are arguably the most extreme generalists in the history of life:
- Thriving on virtually every terrestrial habitat
- Surviving on an enormous range of diets
- Functioning in temperatures from arctic to tropical
- Living in groups from 20 to 10 million

This is made possible by the human capacity for cultural adaptation — we don't just tolerate different environments biologically, we modify them culturally to suit our needs.

---

## The Cost of Generalism

Generalists are outcompeted by specialists in stable, predictable environments. The specialist can exploit a known resource more efficiently. The advantage of generalism is resilience in variable, novel, or changing environments.

This matches the [[Craft/Mastercraft/Intelligence/State of the Art/Complexity/Complex/Complexity/Evolution/Niche Switching]] principle: generalist species are the ones that can shift niches when their current one fails.
