# Monogamy

> A mating system in which one male pairs with one female, either for a breeding season or for life. Monogamy is associated with high levels of parental investment from both sexes — typically when offspring survival requires two caregivers.

---

## Definition

**Monogamy**: a mating system in which one male mates with one female, for either a breeding season (serial monogamy) or for life (lifelong monogamy).

Monogamy is relatively rare among mammals but common in birds — where biparental care substantially improves offspring survival.

---

## Why Monogamy Evolves

Monogamy tends to evolve when:
1. **Offspring survival depends on two parents** — one caregiver isn't enough to raise successful offspring
2. **Resources are defensible** — one male can provide enough resources to incentivize female mate choice based on resource quality
3. **Female dispersal prevents harem formation** — males cannot simultaneously monopolize multiple females

In humans, monogamy is one component of a variable mating system — present in many but not all societies.

---

## The Human Case

Humans are unusual: we have features associated with both monogamy (biparental care, concealed ovulation, pair-bonding) and polygyny (male-male competition, sexual size dimorphism). This suggests an evolutionary history with both systems playing roles at different times and in different populations.
