# Sensu Stricto

> “In the narrow sense.” Latin technical term used in taxonomy and biology to specify that a name is being used in its most restricted, precise definition. Contrasted with sensu lato (“in the broad sense”).

---

## Definition

**Sensu stricto** (s.s.) = “in the strict/narrow sense”

When a biological name or concept has multiple possible interpretations at different taxonomic levels, “sensu stricto” specifies the narrowest, most precise usage:

- *Homo sapiens* sensu stricto: anatomically modern humans only
- *Homo sapiens* sensu lato: potentially including archaic human populations

---

## Usage

In taxonomy: when a genus or species name has been applied to different groups over time, “sensu stricto” pins the name to its original, most restricted usage.

In conceptual arguments: “sensu stricto” signals that you are using a term in its precise technical sense, not in a loose colloquial sense.
