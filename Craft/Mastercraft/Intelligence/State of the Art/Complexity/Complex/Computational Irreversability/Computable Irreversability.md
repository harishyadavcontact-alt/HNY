## Core Concepts

### [[Computational Irreducibility]]
The principle that for many computational systems, there is no shortcut to predict outcomes without running every step of the computation.

**Key insights:**
- Even simple rules can produce behavior requiring full computation to predict
- We cannot "jump ahead" because we're not computationally more sophisticated than the systems we study
- Rule 30 cellular automaton is the quintessential example

### [[Craft/Mastercraft/Intelligence/State of the Art/Complexity/Complex/Computational Irreversability/Principle of Computational Equivalence]]
- [ ] Above a low threshold, systems achieve equivalent computational sophistication regardless of their apparent complexity.

**Key claims:**
- Human brains, computers, and simple systems like Rule 30 have equivalent computational capabilities
- Universal computation is ubiquitous in the computational universe
- This is why we cannot outrun computationally irreducible systems

**Evidence:** Rule 110 cellular automaton demonstrates universal computation from simple rules

**Significance:** These pockets make science possible - they're what we can actually predict and study

## Physics Applications

### [[Second Law of Thermodynamics Explained]]
Entropy increase emerges from the interplay between computational irreducibility and observer limitations.

**Mechanism:**
- Initial ordered state is computationally simple to describe
- Evolution encrypts information through irreducible computation
- Computationally bounded observers cannot reverse this encryption
- Therefore entropy appears to increase

**Key insight:** The second law is observer-dependent - sufficiently sophisticated observers might not perceive entropy increase

**Example:** Ice cube melting - easy to predict final state, exponentially harder to reverse-engineer initial configuration

**Connections:** [[Computational Irreducibility]], [[Observer Dependence]], [[Computational Boundedness]]

---

### [[Hypergraph Physics Model]]
Space is composed of discrete "atoms of space" connected in a network (hypergraph).

**Structure:**
- Nodes represent atoms of space
- Connections define spatial relationships
- Evolution occurs through rewriting rules
- Simple initial network grows into complex universe

**Implications:**
- Space itself is discrete, not continuous
- All physical phenomena emerge from network properties
- Gravity arises from network curvature
- Quantum mechanics from branching histories

**Connections:** [[Craft/Mastercraft/Intelligence/State of the Art/Complexity/Complex/Computational Irreversability/The Ruliad]], [[Multiway Systems]], [[Emergent Spacetime]]

---

### [[Multiway Systems]]
Systems with multiple possible evolution paths create branching histories.

**Quantum mechanics interpretation:**
- Multiple ways to apply rewriting rules
- Creates branching structure of possibilities
- Observers themselves branch with the system
- Quantum effects emerge from observer limitations in tracking all branches

**Visualization:** Tree-like structure where each node represents a possible state

**Connections:** [[Hypergraph Physics Model]], [[Observer Dependence]], [[Quantum Mechanics Emergence]]

## The Ruliad

### [[Craft/Mastercraft/Intelligence/State of the Art/Complexity/Complex/Computational Irreversability/The Ruliad]]
The entangled limit of all possible computations - the most fundamental object in the computational universe.

**Definition:** 
- Apply all possible computational rules in all possible ways
- Different computations can produce equivalent states
- These equivalences create elaborate structure
- An inevitable formal consequence of computation itself

**Philosophical significance:**
- The Ruliad is inevitable and complete
- We are embedded within it
- What we observe depends on being observers "like us"
- Laws of physics are what our type of observer perceives

**Connections:** [[Craft/Mastercraft/Intelligence/State of the Art/Complexity/Complex/Computational Irreversability/Principle of Computational Equivalence]], [[Observer Dependence]], [[Computational Universe]]

---

### [[Observer Dependence]]
What we perceive about the universe depends fundamentally on our characteristics as observers.

**Key observer properties:**
- Computationally bounded
- Believe we persist through time
- Experience single thread of consciousness
- Made of different atoms at each moment

**Consequences:**
- Different observers would perceive different physics
- Laws of physics are not absolute but observer-relative
- General relativity and quantum mechanics emerge from these constraints

**Example:** Heat death of universe appears inevitable to bounded observers but not to arbitrarily sophisticated ones

**Connections:** [[Craft/Mastercraft/Intelligence/State of the Art/Complexity/Complex/Computational Irreversability/The Ruliad]], [[Second Law of Thermodynamics Explained]], [[Computational Boundedness]]

---

### [[Computational Boundedness]]
The fundamental limitation that observers cannot perform arbitrarily complex computations.

**Implications for science:**
- We can only access pockets of reducibility
- Limits what laws we can discover
- Makes certain predictions impossible
- Defines boundary between knowable and unknowable

**Relation to value:** Computational reducibility becomes valuable because our bounded nature makes shortcuts essential

## Biology

### [[Biological Evolution Model]]
Evolution can be modeled as adaptive mutation in computational rule space.

**Simple model:**
- Genome = cellular automaton rule
- Mutation = random rule changes
- Fitness = survival time
- Selection = keep longer-lived variants

**Key finding:** Different evolutionary paths discover different "strategies" for fitness

**Connections:** [[Why Evolution Works]], [[Computational Irreducibility]], [[Fitness Function Weakness]]

---

### [[Why Evolution Works]]
Evolution succeeds because fitness functions are computationally weak compared to organism development.

**Mechanism:**
- Network of all possible mutations exists
- Growing organisms involves irreducible computation
- Fitness functions are coarse (live longer, reproduce more)
- Weak fitness can slice through complex mutation network

**Critical insight:** If fitness required solving complex computations, evolution would fail

**Comparison:** Same principle as Second Law - coarse observer (fitness) vs. complex process (development)

**Connections:** [[Biological Evolution Model]], [[Fitness Function Weakness]], [[Computational Irreducibility]]

---

### [[Fitness Function Weakness]]
Biological fitness criteria are computationally simple compared to developmental processes.

**Examples of weak fitness:**
- Live as long as possible
- Maximize offspring
- Survive environmental challenge

**Contrast:** Development involves complex irreducible computation of growth

**Why this matters:** Allows evolution to make progress without solving computationally hard problems

**Connections:** [[Why Evolution Works]], [[Biological Evolution Model]], [[Computational Irreducibility]]

---

### [[Life as Frozen Irreducibility]]
Living systems are arbitrary pieces of computational irreducibility that happen to satisfy fitness constraints.

**Shocking implication:**

- Life is important to us but arbitrary from abstract view

**Challenge:** Can we distinguish adapted systems from random ones?

**Research direction:** Developing general theory of biology

**Connections:** [[Computational Irreducibility]], [[Why Evolution Works]], [[Biology Theory Quest]]

**Current state:**

Neural networks are pieces of computational irreducibility selected to fit objectives.

**Process:**

### [[Simplification Strategy]]
Finding essence by progressively simplifying models.

**Process:**
1. Start with complex system (3D molecules)
2. Simplify (square particles on grid)
3. Simplify more (1D cellular automaton)
4. Find phenomenon still present
5. Study in simplest form

**Example:** Second Law visible in 1D cellular automaton

**Benefit:** Easier to understand mechanism, make general statements

**Connections:** [[Second Law of Thermodynamics Explained]], [[Computational Universe]]
