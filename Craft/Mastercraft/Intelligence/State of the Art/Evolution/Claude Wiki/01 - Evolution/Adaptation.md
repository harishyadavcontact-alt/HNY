# Adaptation

> The accumulation of heritable traits that improve an organism's ability to compete for limited resources in its environment.

---

## What Adaptation Actually Is

Adaptation is what happens when [[Heredity + Selection = Adaptation|heredity is added to selection]]. Selection alone (in the abiotic universe) causes patterns to persist because they are durable. But once you add heredity — the passing of information across generations — you get something fundamentally new: the *accumulation* of beneficial traits over time.

Each generation, traits that improve the organism's ability to accumulate limited stuff (food, mates, territory, light) are slightly favored. Over thousands of generations, these traits compound.

---

## Adaptation ≠ Perfection

Adaptation is always adaptation *to a specific environment*. The [[EEA — Environment of Evolutionary Adaptedness]] is the environment that shaped a trait. When the environment changes faster than adaptation can track, you get [[Mismatch — Designed for an Environment We No Longer Live In|mismatch]].

**Key:** We are adapted to environments that no longer exist. This is the root of [[Hyper-Novelty]].

---

## The Bee in the House

The bee is not maladapted. It is perfectly adapted — for the outside world. Put it in a house and it will beat itself against glass windows trying to escape through what looks like a clear path. The bee is not broken. The environment is wrong.

This is the human condition in the 21st century. See [[The Bee in the House — Analogy]].
