# EEA — Environment of Evolutionary Adaptedness

> The environment in which a trait was shaped by natural selection. Not just the African savanna — we have many EEAs stacked on top of each other.

---

## The Key Insight

Most people think of the EEA as a single environment — the hunter-gatherer African savanna. Heying and Weinstein argue this is too narrow.

We are adapted to *multiple* EEAs, layered historically:

| Stage | Environment | What We Adapted To |
|---|---|---|
| Early tetrapod | Coastal/aquatic to land | Limbs, lungs, terrestrial locomotion |
| Early mammal | Nocturnal predator-rich world | Lactation, fur, warm blood |
| Primate | Forest canopy | Hand dexterity, color vision, social complexity |
| Hunter-gatherer | Open savanna, coasts, forests | Fire, language, cooperation, tools |
| Agriculturalist | Settled farming communities | Grain digestion, delayed gratification, hierarchy |
| Postindustrialist | Urban, anonymous, high-novelty | Still adapting — very incomplete |

---

## Why It Matters for Modern Life

Every domain of modern life can be analyzed as an EEA mismatch:
- We eat like postindustrialists but our guts are hunter-gatherers
- We sleep by artificial light but our circadian systems evolved under sunlight
- We parent in nuclear families but our systems evolved for village child-rearing
