# Heredity

> The passing of information from one generation to the next. The ingredient that transforms abiotic selection into biological evolution.

---

## Why Heredity Is the Critical Ingredient

Selection exists in the abiotic universe. Patterns that are durable, common, and good at accumulating limited stuff persist. But without heredity, there is no *accumulation* of adaptive traits over time.

**The equation:**
> [[Selection Exists Before Biology|Selection]] + [[Craft/Mastercraft/Intelligence/State of the Art/Evolution/Claude Wiki/01 - Evolution/Heredity]] = [[Adaptation]]
> Adaptation = what makes the biotic universe special

Before heredity: selection filters, but nothing *learns*.
After heredity: each generation can build on the last. Traits accumulate. Complexity compounds.

---

## What Is Being Passed

Genetic information (DNA) is the primary vehicle. But heredity is broader:
- **Genetic:** DNA from parent to offspring
- **Epigenetic:** gene expression patterns that can be inherited
- **Cultural:** learned behaviors passed within a generation or across generations (see [[Cultural Transfer of Information]])

Cultural heredity is what makes [[The Campfire — Forge of Ideas|the campfire]] so powerful — it extends heredity beyond biology.

---

## The Transition Point

The moment heredity enters the picture, you cross a threshold:
- Abiotic patterns persist because they are physically stable
- Biotic patterns persist *and improve* because they accumulate adaptive information

This is why [[Why Does the Inorganic Universe Give Rise to the Biotic?|the inorganic universe gives rise to the biotic]].
