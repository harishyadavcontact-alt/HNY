# Natural Selection

> The mechanism by which heritable traits that increase reproductive success become more common in a population over generations.

---

## Core Mechanism

Selection acting on heritable variation over time. Organisms with traits better suited to their environment leave more offspring. Those traits spread through the population.

Three requirements:
1. **Variation** — individuals differ
2. **Heritability** — differences are passed on
3. **Differential reproduction** — some variants reproduce more

---

## Important Distinction

Natural selection is often confused with [[Craft/Mastercraft/Intelligence/State of the Art/Evolution/Claude Wiki/01 - Evolution/Fitness]] measured by [[Reproductive Success]]. But reproductive success is only a *proxy* for true fitness — it works 95% of the time and fails in important edge cases (e.g. sterile worker ants carrying the queen's genes).

True fitness = lodging your [[Lineage vs Individual|lineage]] as far into the future as possible, not just your direct descendants.

---

## Selection Before Biology

Selection is not unique to biology. The [[4 Properties of Pattern Prevalence]] show that selection — the force that determines which patterns become common — exists in the [[Abiotic Universe — Competition as Physics|abiotic universe]] before any life exists.

What makes biology special is [[Heredity + Selection = Adaptation|heredity added to selection]].
