# All True Narratives Must Reconcile

> When two things that both claim to be true don't fit together, you don't get to say "maybe they just don't fit." You are required to make them fit over time.

---

## The Principle

> "All true narratives must reconcile. When you have two things that don't reconcile, whether it's between two cultures or two disciplines, we don't allow ourselves the cheat of saying, 'well, maybe they just don't fit.' We say we are required to make them fit over time. That is an important, important principle."
> — Bret Weinstein, Sophia Lecture 2

---

## What This Means in Practice

If you have two well-supported models that make contradictory predictions:
1. You **cannot** ignore the contradiction
2. You **cannot** say they apply in different domains and leave it at that
3. You are **required** to find the deeper model that reconciles them

This is what drove Einstein's search for a unified field theory. This is what drives the search for a "theory of everything" in physics. This is what consilience demands.

---

## Between Cultures and Disciplines

The principle applies beyond science:
- If ancient wisdom and modern medicine contradict each other, that is a signal — not a reason to dismiss one
- If evolutionary biology and the humanities seem to have nothing to say to each other, that is a problem — not an acceptable state
- The integration of sciences and humanities is not optional; the "two cultures" split is a modern prejudice, not a fundamental truth

---

## Relationship to Paradox

A paradox is a failure of reconciliation. Two things that seem true but cannot coexist in the current model. [[Paradox as Signal — X on the Map|The paradox is the X on the map]] — it tells you exactly where the required reconciliation needs to happen.
