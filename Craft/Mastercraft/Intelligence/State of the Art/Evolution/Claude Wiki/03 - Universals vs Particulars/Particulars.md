# Particulars

> The specific, local instantiation of a universal pattern in a given context, material, or history. Not less real than universals — but not generalizable.

---

## Definition

A particular is how a universal pattern manifests in a specific instance. It is always:
- Context-dependent
- History-dependent
- Unique to its specific circumstances

The nautilus shell on a specific beach is a particular. The Fibonacci series it instantiates is a [[Craft/Mastercraft/Intelligence/State of the Art/Evolution/Claude Wiki/03 - Universals vs Particulars/Universals|universal]].

---

## Key Examples

**The Nautilus Shell:**
- The specific shell, on this planet, made of calcium carbonate, with these exact dimensions = particular
- The Fibonacci spiral it embodies = universal
- "What we have done with gold" = particular; gold itself = universal

**The Sagrada Familia Staircase:**
> "There is a spiral staircase at the Sagrada Familia in Barcelona — the fever dream of Gaudí. The fact that there is a spiral staircase that looks like that in Barcelona today tells us we can imagine there will be spiral staircases that look like that elsewhere in the universe. Because the math in this is universal, even though the instantiation of it is not."
> — Heather Heying, Sophia Lecture 3

Gaudí's staircase: a particular. The mathematical spiral it follows: a universal. If there is sentient life building structures elsewhere, they may find the same mathematical form — but it will look completely different.

**Evolution on Earth:**
- That life evolves by selection + heredity = universal (any biotic universe will do this)
- That life on Earth uses DNA = particular (that specific chemistry happened here)
- That humans have two eyes = particular; that sentient life will have some sensory input = universal

---

## The Danger of Confusion

Mistaking a particular for a universal:
- "What is natural for humans is natural for all beings" — ethnocentrism, essentialism
- "Our cultural practices are universal human values" — colonialism, universalizing error

Mistaking a universal for a particular:
- "The laws of physics are just our cultural construction" — relativism
- "Mathematics is invented, not discovered" — (contested, but this view denies universals)
