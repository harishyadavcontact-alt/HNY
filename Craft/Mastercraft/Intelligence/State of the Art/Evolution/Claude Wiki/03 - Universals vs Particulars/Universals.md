# Universals

> Patterns that hold everywhere in the universe, regardless of context, history, or instantiation. The laws of physics and mathematics are the clearest examples.

---

## Definition

A universal is a pattern or truth that:
1. Holds everywhere in the universe, not just here
2. Is not dependent on the specific materials, history, or context
3. Would be discovered by any sufficiently intelligent observer, anywhere

> "We spoke of the universals — such as the laws of physics and math as represented in this shell, but not by this shell."
> — Heather Heying, Sophia Lecture 3

The shell represents the Fibonacci series. The Fibonacci series itself is universal. The shell is a [[Particulars|particular]].

---

## Key Examples

**Fibonacci Series (1, 1, 2, 3, 5, 8, 13...):**
- Each number is the sum of the previous two
- Appears in shell spirals, plant growth, galaxy spirals, DNA structure
- *Not because nature copies itself* — because the Fibonacci pattern is a solution to certain growth optimization problems, and those problems appear everywhere
- Will have been discovered by sentient life everywhere in the universe

**Gold (atomic number 79):**
- Specific atomic properties make gold behave in specific ways regardless of where in the universe
- Gold formed only in supernovae — universal mechanism
- The *element* is universal; what any civilization does with gold is a particular

**Laws of Physics:**
- Gravity, electromagnetism, thermodynamics — same everywhere
- Any physics experiment done correctly produces the same result regardless of who does it, where, or when

---

## Why Universals Matter

Understanding what is universal vs. what is particular is one of the most powerful tools in science:
- Universal patterns reveal deep structure
- Particular instantiations reveal how that deep structure expresses in specific contexts
- Confusing them leads to cultural relativism (thinking all patterns are particular) or essentialism (thinking all particulars are universal)
