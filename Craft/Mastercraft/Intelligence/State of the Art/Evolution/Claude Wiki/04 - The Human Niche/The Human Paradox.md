# The Human Paradox

> The fundamental rule: jack-of-all-trades = master of none. The fundamental exception: Homo sapiens. We are jacks of nearly every trade and masters of nearly every habitat.

---

## The Rule

"The jack-of-all-trades is the master of none" is one of the most universal principles in ecology, engineering, and biology. A species that tries to occupy every niche ends up outcompeted in each niche by specialists. To be dominant anywhere, you must specialize — sacrificing breadth.

This principle has been observed for 400+ years in print. In ecology, it is axiomatic: generalists survive, specialists thrive.

---

## The Exception

> "Yet somehow here we are, jacks of nearly every trade imaginable, and simultaneously the masters of nearly every habitat on Earth. Our niche is nearly unbounded, and when we do find boundaries, we nearly immediately begin to test them."
> — Heying & Weinstein, *A Hunter-Gatherer's Guide*

Homo sapiens violates the rule. We are not just successful generalists — we are specialist-level performers in virtually every domain we enter. We grow our own food (agricultural specialists), hunt (hunting specialists), build (engineering specialists), navigate (navigational specialists), and do all of them well.
