# Hyper-Novelty

> The defining condition of the modern world: the rate of environmental change has exceeded the rate at which biological and cultural evolution can adapt.

---

## The Term

Coined by Heather Heying and Bret Weinstein:

> "The best, most all-encompassing way to describe our world is hyper-novel. Humans are extraordinarily well adapted to, and equipped for, change. But the rate of change itself is so rapid now that our brains, bodies, and social systems are perpetually out of sync."
> — Heying & Weinstein, *A Hunter-Gatherer's Guide*

---

## The Core Structure

```
Organism = adapted to Environment A (EEA)
Modern World = Environment B (rapidly changing postindustrial)

Rate of change of Environment B > Rate of biological adaptation

∴ Organism is perpetually mismatched
∴ Every new "environment" creates new mismatches faster than old ones are resolved
```

---

## This Is Not a New Problem

The [[EEA — Environment of Evolutionary Adaptedness|EEA concept]] acknowledges that we have always lived in environments different from our ancestral ones. But the *rate* of change is what is unprecedented.

For millions of years, environmental change was slow enough that cultural adaptation (traditions, practices, institutions) could bridge the gap between biological adaptation and the current environment. Now the change is so fast that even culture cannot keep up.

---

## The Consequences

> "Our species' pace of change now outstrips our ability to adapt. We are generating new problems at a new and accelerating rate, and it is making us sick — physically, psychologically, socially, and environmentally."
> — Heying & Weinstein

Manifestations across domains:
- **Physical:** Chronic disease rates, obesity, metabolic syndrome
- **Psychological:** Anxiety, depression, loneliness epidemics
- **Social:** Dissolution of community, family breakdown
- **Environmental:** Ecological destruction

---

## The Hope

The same impulses that created hyper-novelty — human ingenuity, adaptability, exploration — are the only tools capable of navigating it.

> "We are explorers and innovators by design, and the same impulses that have created our troublesome modern condition are the only hope for saving it."
