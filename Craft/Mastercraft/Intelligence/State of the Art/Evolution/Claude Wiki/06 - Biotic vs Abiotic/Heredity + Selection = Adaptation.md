# Heredity + Selection = Adaptation

> The most important equation in biology. Two simple ingredients. One spectacular consequence.

---

## The Equation

```
Selection (exists in abiotic universe)
  + Heredity (passing information across generations)
  = Adaptation (accumulation of traits that enhance competitive success)
```

This is the threshold between the abiotic and biotic universes. Before heredity: selection filters patterns but nothing learns. After heredity: each generation builds on the last. Complexity compounds. Life becomes possible.

---

## Why Selection Alone Is Insufficient

Selection exists in the abiotic universe. Gold is abundant because it's durable and accumulates protons well. Spiral galaxies are common because they're stable and accumulate stars well. These are selection effects — patterns that persist because they "compete" for the universe's limited matter and energy.

But nothing improves. Gold does not become better at being gold over time. A spiral galaxy does not learn to accumulate stars more efficiently across generations.

Because there is no heredity. No information passing. No accumulation of adaptive traits.

---

## Why Heredity Alone Is Insufficient

If you could perfectly copy an organism but there was no selection — no differential survival — every trait would persist regardless of its effects. Nothing would improve or be refined. Random copying errors would accumulate without filter.

---

## The Together Effect: Striving

Once you add heredity to selection, you get something new: **adaptation**. Organisms begin to *accumulate* traits that help them compete for limited stuff. They do not need to understand what they are doing. The objective is built into the system.

This is why the salmon swims upstream. This is why the sea otter breaks into the clam. They are striving toward objectives they do not understand, built to do so by the logic of heredity + selection operating over millions of generations.
