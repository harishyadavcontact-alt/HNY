# Corvids — Intelligence

> Ravens, crows, and jays are birds. They separated from the mammalian line ~300 million years ago. Yet they demonstrate problem-solving, tool use, planning, and social intelligence that rivals primates.

---

## The Lineage

Corvids are passerine birds — songbirds. They are not closely related to humans, primates, or cetaceans. Their last common ancestor with us was a small reptile-like creature hundreds of millions of years ago.

Their brains are structured completely differently from mammalian brains — no neocortex. Yet the outcomes are similar.

---

## What They Do

**Tool use:**
- New Caledonian crows manufacture hooked tools from leaves to extract insects from bark
- They can solve multi-step problems requiring tools in sequence
- They can make tools they have never seen before by generalizing from principles

**Planning:**
- Western scrub jays cache food for the future — planning for times of scarcity
- They re-hide caches when they know they were observed — anticipating theft
- This requires understanding of past (what I buried), present (I was watched), and future (theft will happen)

**Social intelligence:**
- Ravens track social hierarchies and alliances
- They form long-term social bonds
- They engage in apparent reconciliation after conflicts

**Theory of Mind:**
- Evidence that corvids can model what other individuals know — they understand "witnessed vs. unwitnessed" caching

---

## The Convergence Lesson

Corvids prove that the *structure* of intelligent behavior (tool use, planning, theory of mind) does not require a mammalian neocortex. Evolution found a different architectural solution to the same problem.

This is [[Craft/Mastercraft/Intelligence/State of the Art/Evolution/Claude Wiki/01 - Evolution/Convergent Evolution]] at the cognitive level — the same ecological pressures (complex social environment, long life, cultural transmission in some species) selected for similar outcomes through different mechanisms.
