# Whales and Dolphins — Intelligence

> Cetaceans returned to the sea ~50 million years ago. They developed intelligence, culture, and social complexity on a completely different body plan, in a completely different environment.

---

## The Lineage

Cetaceans (whales, dolphins, porpoises) are mammals that returned to the aquatic environment. They are most closely related to hippopotamuses. Their last common ancestor with primates was ~85 million years ago.

Their intelligence evolved entirely in water, without hands, without fire, in an environment with completely different sensory constraints (primarily echolocation and sound, not vision).

---

## What They Do

**Tool use (dolphins):**
- Bottlenose dolphins in Shark Bay, Australia use sponges to protect their snouts while bottom-feeding
- This behavior is taught by mothers to daughters — a specific female lineage
- It is [[Cultural Transfer of Information|culturally transmitted]], not genetically inherited

**Cultural songs (humpback whales):**
- Male humpbacks sing complex songs that change over time
- New "hit songs" spread across ocean basins — cultural transmission at oceanic scale
- Songs from one population are adopted and modified by distant populations

**Social complexity:**
- Bottlenose dolphins have fission-fusion societies — flexible social groupings with long-term individual recognition
- They form alliances of alliances (second-order coalitions) — some of the most complex social structures known outside of humans

**Self-recognition:**
- Dolphins pass the mirror test — evidence of self-awareness

---

## The Water Constraint

Cetacean intelligence developed *without*:
- Hands (no tool manufacture of the primate kind)
- Fire
- Visual-dominant sensory world

Yet convergently produced:
- Cultural transmission
- Social complexity
- Possibly theory of mind

This demonstrates that intelligence is not a product of any specific physical or sensory configuration — it is the product of complex social demands.
