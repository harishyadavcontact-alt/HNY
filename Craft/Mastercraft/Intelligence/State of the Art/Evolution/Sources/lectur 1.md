Source guide

This source introduces a lecture series by evolutionary biologists Bret Weinstein and Heather Heying at Ralston College, emphasizing a critique of **reductionist, data-driven "pseudo-science"** and advocating for an approach that embraces **courage, creativity, curiosity, and uncertainty**. Speaker Stephen Blackwood first outlines Ralston College's core commitments to **seeking the truth, freedom of inquiry, beauty, and fellowship**, presenting the college as an endeavor to restore the **universality of thinking** by bridging the perceived gap between the sciences and the humanities. Dr. Heying then begins her talk, establishing the concept of **consilience**—the necessity of reconciling evidence from multiple fields (molecular, morphological, developmental, and behavioral) to infer accurate evolutionary history, since relying on only one type of evidence is a "mistake" that can lead to incorrect conclusions about relationships. Ultimately, the speakers aim to redefine science as a field that champions **wonder and free expression**, rejecting the idea of a universal "best" or certainty in favor of understanding **evolution as descent with modification** and a constant response to change.
