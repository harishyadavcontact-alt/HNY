# Propaganda

# The Room

Propaganda is information that is spread deliberately to influence public opinion. It can be used for good or bad purposes, but it is often used to manipulate people and to control their behavior.

Propaganda is communication that is primarily used to influence or persuade an audience to further an agenda, which may not be objective and may be selectively presenting facts to encourage a particular synthesis or perception, or using loaded language to produce an emotional rather than a rational response to the information that is being presented.

Propaganda is often used to promote a particular political ideology, religion, or product. It can also be used to create fear or hatred of a particular group of people.

Propaganda  
• Exploiting Emotional VulnerabilitiesIdentifying and targeting emotional weaknesses to manipulate the individual’s emotions.  
• Repetition and IndoctrinationRepeatedly presenting the same beliefs and messages to reinforce acceptance and internalization.  
• Selective reporting  
◦ **:** Only reporting information that supports the desired agenda, while ignoring or downplaying information that contradicts it.  
• Fake Testimonials  
Con men may provide fake testimonials or references from supposed satisfied customers or investors.  
◦  
1. Enlisting Accomplices:  
In some cases, con men may use accomplices to play roles or provide fake testimonials.  
◦ Testimonial  
This strategy uses endorsements from celebrities or experts to make a product or idea seem more credible. For example, a company might use this strategy by having a celebrity say their product is “the best thing since sliced bread.”*  
• Loaded language  
◦ Using words that have strong emotional associations to evoke a particular response from the audience.  
• Repetition  
◦ Repeating a message over and over again can make it seem more credible.  
• Stigma  
◦ **:** Creating a negative association with a particular group of people or idea.  
• GaslightingManipulating or distorting reality to make the individual doubt their own perceptions, memories, and sanity.  
• Testimonials  
◦ **:** Using people who are seen as experts or authorities to endorse a product or idea.  
• Bandwagon appeal  
◦ Suggesting that everyone else is doing something, so you should too.  
• Fear-mongering  
◦ Using fear to persuade people to take a particular action.
