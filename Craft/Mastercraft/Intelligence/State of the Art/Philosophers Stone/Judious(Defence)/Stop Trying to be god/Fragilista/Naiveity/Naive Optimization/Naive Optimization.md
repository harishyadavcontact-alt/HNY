Naive optimization, in the context of Taleb’s work, is understood as a manifestation of the **Fragilista’s** delusion, wherein an attempt to create predicted efficiency or stability (usually through centralized, top-down planning) ignores hidden risks, nonlinear consequences, and the critical need for redundancies. This process is tied to modern complexity and is ultimately fragility-inducing.

### I. The Problem: Naive Optimization and Naive Interventionism

Naive optimization is the intellectual error of applying simplistic, often linear, models to complex, nonlinear reality, inevitably leading to fragility and exposure to Black Swans.

### A. Definitions and Core Flaws

1. **Naive Interventionism and Iatrogenics:** The urge to help or intervene is called “naive interventionism”. This results in net loss, known as iatrogenics, defined as “the (usually hidden or delayed) damage from treatment in excess of the benefits”. Anything in which there is naive interventionism will have iatrogenics.

1. **The Fragilista and Naive Rationalism:** The fragilista, often wearing a suit and tie, is someone who “causes fragility because he thinks he understands what’s going on”. They fall for “the Soviet-Harvard delusion, the (unscientific) overestimation of the reach of scientific knowledge”. They mistake the unknown for the nonexistent and believe that “the reasons behind things are automatically accessible to him”.

1. **Linear Simplification and the Procrustean Bed:** Naive optimization involves simplifying the nonlinear world by treating it as linear, which is a distortion. This is called the “Procrustean bed”. Simplifications fail and cause the most damage when “something nonlinear is simplified with the linear as a substitute”. Naive interventionism, rooted in the fear of randomness and love of smoothness, “blow[s] them up with our fear of randomness and love of smoothness”.

1. **Denial of Antifragility:** Naive intervention corresponds to “the denial of antifragility”. It actively harms systems by “suppressing randomness and volatility”. Complex systems are weakened, even killed, “when deprived of stressors”.

1. **The Forecasting Failure:** Naive optimization relies on prediction, which is fundamentally flawed, especially in “Extremistan”. Corporate and government projections suffer from the flaw of not including a possible error rate in their scenarios. The “accuracy in your forecast matters far more than the forecast itself”.
