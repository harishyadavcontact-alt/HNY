### Examples of Cultural Transmission

**Bottlenose dolphins (Shark Bay):** Females wear sponges on noses to protect while foraging on seafloor—transmitted down matrilineal lines, not laterally

**Caledonian crows:** Tool modification shows cumulative cultural evolution—new tools replace old ones just like human archaeological record

**Spinner dolphins:** 16-28 individuals execute synchronized geometric hunting at night using echolocation—pairs enter feeding circle in choreographed sequence

**Baboons:** Theory of mind—track two independent social hierarchies (male and female), understand rules, predict others' behavior, react to rule-breaking
