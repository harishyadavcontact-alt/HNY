**Maxim:** Go into nature; take facts into your own hands; observe directly rather than through mediation.**Attribution:** [SOURCE]

**Central Argument:**  
Science requires direct engagement. “Go into nature, take the facts into your own hands, look and see for yourself.” This is not theory construction but direct observation. Direct engagement maintains connection to reality. This operationalizes observational priority.

**Source:** [EXPLICIT] Lecture 3/4, attributed to Louis Agassiz - “Go into nature, take the facts into your own hands, look and see for yourself.”

**Epistemological Status:** This is the practical dimension of observational priority. It operationalizes epistemic groundedness.

**Note:** While attributed to Agassiz, it is cited as representing the framework’s methodological requirement.
