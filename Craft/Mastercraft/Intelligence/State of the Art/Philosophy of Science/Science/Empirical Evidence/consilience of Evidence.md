**Maxim:** Universal phenomena require empirical evidence; particular phenomena require consilience


- TYPES OF EVIDENCE IN EVOLUTIONARY BIOLOGY**
	- **A. Four Primary Types (Plus One Rare)**
		**1. MOLECULAR Evidence**
			- DNA sequences: G (guanine), A (adenine), T (thymine), C (cytosine)
			- **Human genome: ~3.1 billion base pairs**
			- Lots of data, but tedious
			- "Really tedious. It's not interesting to anyone"
			
			- Molecular revolution started in 1990s
			
			- "Gene jocks" rely on this alone = mistake
			
			**Problems with molecular-only:**
			
			- Can generate huge amount of data
			
			- "Plug them into a machine and honestly cross our fingers"
			
			- "Hope that it does the right thing"
			
			- Machine spits stuff back out
			
			- "Ah, now we have relationships"
			
			- **Many reasons to be concerned using only molecular data**
	
		**2. MORPHOLOGICAL Evidence**
			
			- **Physical structures (phenotype, not genotype)**
			
			- Gills, jaws, wings, brains
			
			- **Including fossils**
			
			- Gets fractal: can measure parts of brains, margins, connections, branches
			
			- "What people actually want to look at"
			
			**Characters include:**
			
			- Bones and muscles
			
			- Flight structures
			
			- Skeletal architecture
			
			- External features
			
			- Internal anatomy
			
		**3. DEVELOPMENTAL Evidence**
			
			- **How organisms develop from embryos (ontogeny)**
			
			- Embryological patterns
			
			- Individual development through life
			
			- Von Baer's Law (not Haeckel's false claim)
			
			- Provides route into inferring history
			
		**4. BEHAVIORAL Evidence**
			
			- Actions and patterns
			
			- Social behaviors
			
			- (Discussed more in second lecture)
			
			- Often overlooked but crucial
			
		**5. SYMBOLIC Evidence (rarely used)**
			- Languages and symbolic systems
			- Languages evolve like organisms
			- Can generate phylogenies of language
			- "Fascinating"
			- Indo-European language families
			- Anyone who speaks these languages knows we can interpret relationships
	**What we prefer:**

		``- Molecular characters present
		
		- Some morphological characters
		
		- Maybe developmental characters
		
		- Maybe behavioural characters
		
		- **Consilience between different kinds of evidence**
		
		**Consilience = antidote to reductionism:**
		- Only by drawing from molecular, morphological, developmental clues
		
		- Sometimes even symbolic evidence
		
		- Do we build robust scientific understanding
		
		- Independent lines converging = confidence
``


**Central Argument:**  
This distinction is critical. Confusing these standards is a major source of error. Demanding experimental repeatability for historical phenomena is a category error. Treating single evidence lines as definitive for history is a category error. Each type of phenomenon has appropriate evidence standards.

**Epistemological Status:** This is the most important methodological distinction in the framework. It determines what evidence is appropriate for what question.

**Note:** This principle, while not explicitly stated, is central to understanding the framework and is strongly implied by the universal/particular distinction.
