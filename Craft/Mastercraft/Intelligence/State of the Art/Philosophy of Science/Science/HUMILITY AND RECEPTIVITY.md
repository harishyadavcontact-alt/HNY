**Maxim:** Approach nature with openness to abandoning all preconceptions.

**Central Argument:**  
The proper methodological stance is humility and receptivity. Approach facts “as a little child.” Be “prepared to give up every preconceived notion.” “Follow humbly wherever and to whatever abysses nature leads.” This stance permits following evidence even into territories that contradict prior beliefs. It contrasts with defensive positions that protect existing frameworks.

**Source:** [EXPLICIT] Lecture 3/4, cited from Thomas Henry Huxley - “Sit down before a fact as a little child. Be prepared to give up every preconceived notion. Follow humbly wherever and to whatever abysses nature leads, or you shall learn nothing.”

**Epistemological Status:** This is the emotional/attitudinal dimension of epistemology. It is required for genuine learning.

**Note:** This quote is attributed to Huxley but is cited by Heying as representing the framework’s epistemological stance.
