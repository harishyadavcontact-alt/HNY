**Maxim:** Deep evolutionary history requires evidence that preserves information over geological time.**Attribution:** [SOURCE]

**Central Argument:**  
When inferring relationships between species across millions of years, evidence must come from sources that preserve information over geological timescales. Molecular sequences preserve accumulated changes. Fossils provide direct evidence of past forms. Morphology shows homologies indicating common ancestry. These preserve historical signal over deep time.

**Source:** [EXPLICIT] Lecture 3 (Heather Heying) - “When you try to use behavioral characters to infer deep history it is of course more challenging because behavior changes rapidly. Behavior doesn’t require a generation change, a reproductive event to make a change in a lineage.”

**Implication:** Behavior is unsuitable for deep history because it changes too fast to be historical signal.

**Epistemological Status:** This specifies what evidence types preserve information over macroevolutionary timescales.
