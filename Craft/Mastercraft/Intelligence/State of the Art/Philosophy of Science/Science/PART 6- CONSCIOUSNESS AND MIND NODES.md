### NODE 6.1: CONSCIOUSNESS OPERATIONALLY DEFINED AXIOM

**Maxim:** Consciousness is cognition packaged for exchange; communicability is the criterion.**Attribution:** [SOURCE]

**Central Argument:**  
Consciousness is operationally defined as communicable cognition. “Conscious thoughts are those that can be communicated to others.” This makes consciousness scientifically tractable by focusing on measurable communicability rather than subjective experience. The authors claim this captures “the epicenter of what people mean when describing a thought as conscious.”

**Source:** [EXPLICIT] Hunter-Gatherer’s Guide to the 21st Century, Chapter 1 - “Conscious thoughts are those that can be communicated to others. We define consciousness, therefore, as ‘that fraction of cognition that is packaged for exchange.’ This is no trick. We have not chosen a definition to make an intractable question simple. We have chosen the definition at the epicenter of what people mean when describing a thought as ‘conscious.’”

**Epistemological Status:** This operationalizes consciousness in scientific terms. It shifts focus from inner subjective experience (not directly measurable) to communicability (observable and testable).

### NODE 6.2: SHARED CONSCIOUSNESS AS EMERGENT PROPERTY AXIOM

**Maxim:** Shared consciousness emerges between people as collective property, not reducible to individuals.**Attribution:** [SOURCE]

**Central Argument:**  
Consciousness is not only individual but emerges at the collective level. “Shared consciousness is an emergent, intangible space between people, where concepts are lodged and co-cultivated.” Each participant has a distinct perspective on this space, but “the space is a property of the collective”—not reducible to any individual mind. Consciousness thus has both individual and collective aspects.

**Source:** [EXPLICIT] Hunter-Gatherer’s Guide to the 21st Century, Chapter 1 - “Shared consciousness is an emergent, intangible space between people, where concepts are lodged and co-cultivated. Each participant has a distinct perspective on the space, much as each witness to a physical event will have a somewhat different vantage point, but the space is a property of the collective.”

**Epistemological Status:** This is a principle about consciousness at different scales: individual vs. emergent collective. It explains how collective understanding emerges.

**Relationship to NODE 6.1:** Individual consciousness (communicable cognition) combines to form emergent shared consciousness at collective level.

### NODE 6.3: THEORY OF MIND RECOGNITION AXIOM

**Maxim:** Theory of mind is recognizing that other minds have different mental contents.**Attribution:** [SOURCE]

**Central Argument:**  
Theory of mind is the capacity to recognize that another mind has different beliefs, desires, and models of reality. “They have the ability to discern that what they think is true about something is not necessarily what someone else thinks is true about something.” This is foundational for understanding other minds and coordinating with them.

**Source:** [EXPLICIT] Lecture 3/4 (Heather Heying) - “They have the ability to discern that what they think is true about something is not necessarily what someone else, some other baboon thinks is true about something.”

**Epistemological Status:** This is foundational to social cognition. It enables prediction and coordination with other minds.

**Application:** Baboons demonstrate theory of mind: they track status of multiple other baboons and form coalitions based on models of how others will respond.

**Relationship to NODE 6.2:** Theory of mind enables shared consciousness. Without recognizing that other minds have different contents, shared consciousness cannot emerge.

### NODE 6.4: CONSCIOUSNESS & SOCIAL COMPLEXITY CONNECTION AXIOM

**Maxim:** Consciousness and theory of mind enable complex social structures through mutual recognition.**Attribution:** [INFERRED from source]

**Central Argument:**  
The combination of consciousness (NODE 6.1, 6.2) and theory of mind (NODE 6.3) enables social complexity. Shared consciousness emerges when minds recognize each other through theory of mind. This enables social structures more complex than individual organisms can achieve.

**Source Support:** [EXPLICIT] Lecture 3/4 (Heather Heying) - Baboon example shows theory of mind enabling complex social structures based on mutual recognition and tracking.

**Epistemological Status:** This principle connects individual cognition to social phenomena. It explains emergence of collective understanding.
