### NODE 7.1: PLASTICITY AXIOM

**Maxim:** Development is fundamentally responsive to environment; same genotype produces different phenotypes.**Attribution:** [SOURCE]

**Central Argument:**  
Plasticity is “the ability to change even regardless of whether or not there is difference in the genes.” The same genetic sequence can produce different developmental outcomes based on environmental input. This means development is fundamentally interactive, not deterministic from genes alone.

**Source:** [EXPLICIT] Lecture 3/4 (Heather Heying) - “This is just a term that we use in biology to refer to the ability to change um even even regardless of whether or not there is difference in the genes.”

**Epistemological Status:** This is a principle about how organisms develop. It explains gene-environment interaction.

### NODE 7.2: NEURONAL PRUNING MECHANISM AXIOM

**Maxim:** Development overproduces connections; experience selectively eliminates up to 70% through cell death.**Attribution:** [SOURCE]

**Central Argument:**  
The mechanism of plasticity is: overproduction of neural connections in infancy, followed by experience-based selective elimination of up to seventy percent of connections by adulthood. This is not a failure or messy loss but optimal strategy: “it’s better to overproduce and then see what works. So it’s a kind of exploration.” Experience literally shapes neural structure through selective preservation and elimination.

**Source:** [EXPLICIT] Lecture 3/4 (Heather Heying) - “Then the neurons are selectively eliminated through cell death such that by the adulthood up to 70% of the connections in the neurons that you made as a tiny baby and as a child have died back. And that’s not an accident and that’s not tragic and it’s not a messy loss that we could do without because it’s better to overproduce and then see what works. So it’s a kind of exploration.”

**Epistemological Status:** This is the mechanistic explanation for plasticity. It shows how learning becomes embodied in neural structure.

**Implication:** Experience shapes not just behavior but neural architecture itself.

### NODE 7.3: LEARNING = STRUCTURAL CHANGE AXIOM

**Maxim:** What you learn changes your neural structure through selective elimination of connections.**Attribution:** [INFERRED from source]

**Central Argument:**  
Learning is not merely behavioral change but structural change. What you learn during development is embodied in which neural connections are preserved and which are eliminated. This means learning is literally embodied in your brain structure.

**Source Support:** [EXPLICIT] NODE 7.2 (neuronal pruning) combined with NODE 7.1 (development responsive to environment).

**Epistemological Status:** This principle connects learning (cognitive) to structure (physical). It explains the embodiment of knowledge.

**Implication:** Learning from experience cannot be reduced to behavioral flexibility; it involves actual structural changes in neural organization.
