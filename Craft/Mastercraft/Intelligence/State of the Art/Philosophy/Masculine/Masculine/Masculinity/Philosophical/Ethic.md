Private Ethics vs. Public Ethics and Principles

The distinction lies primarily in the scaling and the application of symmetry to avoid the transfer of fragility when dealing with the collective.  

Public Ethics (The Problem of Scale): Ethics degrade as the "we" (the collective) becomes too large. Universalism (like Kant’s categorical imperative) can be dangerous when it ignores scaling. ◦

- Mandate of Sincerity: If your private life conflicts with your intellectual opinion, it cancels your intellectual ideas, not your private life. ◦

- Public Office Protocol: It is downright unethical to use public office for enrichment. A drastic solution is that anyone going into public service should not be allowed to subsequently earn more from any commercial activity than the income of the highest paid civil servant (a voluntary cap)

- Private Ethics: Private life is concerned with localized ethical rules (e.g., family, community) that work best because they are less abstract

- **The Silver Rule: Do not treat others the way you would not like them to treat you**
    
    - The Golden Rule: Do unto others as you would have them do unto you.
    
    - The Golden/Silver Rule: These rules are most robust at the local scale. They apply to individuals, families, and tribes
    
    - Mind your own business and do not decide what is good for others that we know with more clarity what is bad than what is good
