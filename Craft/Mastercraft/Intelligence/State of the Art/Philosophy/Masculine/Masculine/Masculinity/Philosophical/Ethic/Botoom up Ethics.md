The idea that **"if your private actions do not generalize, then you cannot have general ideas"** is a fundamental tenet of Taleb’s ethics, specifically addressing the divergence between "cheap talk" (tawk) and action. It posits that an individual's private conduct is the only reliable signal of the validity and sincerity of their public intellectual positions.

I. The Information Signal

Taleb treats this principle as a matter of information reliability rather than just moralizing.

• **Messenger Reliability:** If a person advocates for a certain rule but refuses to live by it, they are signaling that the idea is either impractical or fraudulent.

• **The Car Salesman Heuristic:** A car salesman who tries to sell you a Detroit-made vehicle while personally driving a Honda is providing a "signal" that the product he is touting has a problem.

• **Cancellation of Ideas:** When a private life conflicts with an intellectual opinion, the conflict does not justify the private life; instead, it effectively "cancels" the intellectual idea.

II. Rejection of Vapid Universalism

The concept serves as a filter against "universalism," which Taleb describes as an abstract, often disastrous approach to the world that ignores local reality and personal responsibility.

• **Local Over Global:** Humans are local and practical animals; therefore, for an idea to be "general," it must first survive the test of being implemented by the individual.

• **The Systematic Solution Trap:** Many "Intellectual Yet Idiots" (IYIs) claim to support broad social justice while refusing to perform local acts of virtue.

• **Pseudo-Virtue:** Claiming to help "the poor" as an abstract collective while belittling individual waiters or neighbors is a form of immoral "virtue merchandising".


• **Ethical Inversion:** Modernity is plagued by people who "fit ethics to their profession" (accommodating their behavior to their job) rather than finding a profession that fits their inherent ethics.

IV. The Essence of Virtue

Virtue is defined by what you do when nobody is looking, not by the "virtue signaling" of public statements or organized charities.

• **Courage is Unfakeable:** While most virtues can be faked with words, courage (risk-taking) is the only virtue that requires literal skin in the game.

• **Unpopular Virtue:** The highest form of virtue is taking a position that is penalized by the common discourse, as it signals genuine behavior through personal risk.
