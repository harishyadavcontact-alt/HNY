- • Agit Prop
    
    “Agitated propaganda” refers to a form of communication or messaging that is intended to provoke strong emotions or agitation in the audience. This type of propaganda often uses highly charged language, sensationalized imagery, or exaggerated claims to manipulate people’s emotions and beliefs. The goal of agitated propaganda is usually to mobilize support for a particular cause, ideology, or leader by appealing to people’s fears, prejudices, or desires. This term is commonly used in political contexts to describe propaganda that seeks to incite anger, outrage, or enthusiasm in order to rally people to a specific cause or to discredit an opposing viewpoint.  
    

- • Use of the press:
    
    Bolshevik strategy from the beginning was to gain access to the primary medium of dissemination of information in Russia: the press  
    

- • Oral-agitation networks:
    
    The Bolshevik leadership understood that to build a lasting regime, they would need to win the support of the mass population of Russian peasants. To do this, Lenin organized a Communist party that attracted demobilized soldiers and others to become supporters of the Bolshevik ideology, dressed up in uniforms and sent to travel the countryside as agitators to the peasants. The oral-agitation networks established a presence in the isolated rural areas of Russia, expanding Communist power.  
    •
    

- Agitational trains and ships:
    
    To expand the reach of the oral-agitation networks, the Bolsheviks pioneered using modern transportation to reach deeper into Russia. The trains and ships carried agitators armed with leaflets, posters, and other various forms of agitprop. Train cars included a garage of motorcycles and cars in order for propaganda materials to reach the rural towns not located near rail lines. The agitational trains expanded the reach of agitators into Eastern Europe and allowed for the establishment of agitprop stations, consisting of libraries of propaganda material. The trains were also equipped with radios, and their own printing press, so they could report to Moscow the political climate of the given region, and receive instruction on how to custom print propaganda on the spot to better take advantage of the situation.  
    •
    

- Literacy campaign:
    
    The peasant society of Russia in 1917 was largely illiterate making it difficult to reach them through printed agitprop. The People’s Commissariat of Enlightenment was established to spearhead the war on illiteracy. Instructors were trained in 1919 and sent to the countryside to create more instructors and expand the operation into a network of literacy centers. New textbooks were created, explaining Bolshevik ideology to the newly literate members of Soviet society, and the literacy training in the army was expanded.
    

- **Simplification**
    
    1. : Agitprop messages are often simple and straightforward, designed to appeal to a broad audience and convey a clear, easy-to-understand message.  
    •
    

- **Emotional Appeal**
    
    1. : Agitprop relies heavily on emotional appeal, aiming to evoke strong feelings such as anger, fear, or excitement in the audience to motivate them to action.  
    •
    

- **Repetition**
    
    1. : Agitprop messages are often repeated frequently to reinforce the desired message and make it more memorable to the audience.  
    •
    

- **Symbolism**
    
    1. : Agitprop often uses powerful symbols and imagery to convey its message, such as flags, slogans, or iconic figures.  
    •
    

- **Demonization**
    
    1. : Agitprop may seek to demonize opponents or portray them in a negative light to rally support for a particular cause or ideology.  
    •
    

- **Slogans and Catchphrases**
    
    1. : Agitprop often uses catchy slogans and catchphrases that are easy to remember and repeat, helping to spread the message widely.  
    •
    

- **Use of Mass Media**
    
    1. : Agitprop often utilizes mass media channels such as newspapers, radio, television, and social media to reach a large audience quickly and effectively.  
    •
    

- **Cultural Events**
    
    1. : Agitprop can also involve the organization of cultural events such as plays, concerts, or art exhibitions to promote political messages and mobilize support.
