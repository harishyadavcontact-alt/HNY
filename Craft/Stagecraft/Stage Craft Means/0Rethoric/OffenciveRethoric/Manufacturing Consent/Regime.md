1. **Mudslinging:** The practice of making malicious or damaging accusations against opponents in order to damage their reputation.

1. **Smear Campaign:** A coordinated effort to spread negative or false information about a candidate or public figure.

1. **Character Assassination:** Deliberately damaging someone’s reputation, often through false or exaggerated claims.

1. **Negative Campaigning:** Focusing on criticizing opponents rather than promoting one’s own policies or qualities.

1. **Dog Whistle Politics:** Using coded language or messages that have a deeper, often prejudiced meaning to specific groups.

1. **Dirty Tricks:** Unethical tactics or strategies used to gain an advantage or undermine opponents.

1. **Astroturfing:** Creating fake grassroots movements or organizations to promote a particular agenda.

1. **Sleaze Factor:** The perceived level of unethical behavior or scandal associated with a politician or campaign.

1. **Swiftboating:** A form of political attack that involves spreading false or misleading information to discredit a candidate’s military service or record.

1. **Hit Piece:** A critical or defamatory article or story intended to harm a person’s reputation.

1. **Whisper Campaign:** Spreading rumors or false information in a secretive or indirect manner.

1. **Opposition Research:** Gathering information about opponents to identify weaknesses and vulnerabilities.

1. **Dirt File:** A collection of negative information, often confidential, used to tarnish opponents.

1. **Doublespeak:** Using language that may sound positive but is actually meant to deceive or manipulate.

1. **Dirty Money:** Illegitimate or unethical funding used to support a campaign or political activity.

1. **Fake News:** False or misleading information presented as real news to influence public opinion.

1. **Political Espionage:** Illegally gathering information about opponents to gain a strategic advantage.

1. **False Flag Operation:** Creating a deceptive situation to make it appear as if another party is responsible for certain actions.

1. **Innuendo:** A subtle or indirect hint suggesting something negative without directly stating it.

1. **Whistleblower:** A person who exposes unethical or illegal activities within an organization or government.
