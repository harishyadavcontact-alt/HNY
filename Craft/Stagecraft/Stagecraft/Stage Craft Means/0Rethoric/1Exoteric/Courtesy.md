### Courtesy and Cultural Respect

**Technical Definition:** Courtesy as strategic navigation of cultural algorithms that maximizes social optionality while minimizing unnecessary friction.

**Mechanical Definition:** The process of identifying, respecting, and selectively implementing cultural norms based on their Lindy effect (time-tested value) and ethical alignment.

**Functional Definition:** Courtesy serves to reduce transaction costs in social interactions, signal in-group awareness, and create preferential treatment opportunities.

**Operational Definition:** Measured by the ability to move between different cultural contexts without creating resistance or being identified as an outsider.

- Navigate social norms strategically while maintaining personal integrity

- Respect traditions with proven longevity (Lindy Effect) while rejecting harmful practices

- Cultural fluency creates optionality across multiple social domains

- Eliminate discourtesy first before adding courteous behaviors

- Honor heritage conditionally based on its proven value and ethical alignment

- Stand against cultural deadweight that creates fragility rather than antifragility

- When traditions conflict with ethics, choose ethics but minimize unnecessary confrontation

- When navigating unfamiliar cultures, observe before participating and default to respectful restraint
