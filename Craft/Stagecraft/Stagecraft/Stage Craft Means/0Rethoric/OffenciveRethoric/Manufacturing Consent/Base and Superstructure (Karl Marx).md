- In Marxist theory, society is divided into the _economic base_ (the forces and relations of production) and the _superstructure_ (culture, institutions, politics, and ideology). The base shapes the superstructure, meaning that the economic structure of society determines its ideological and cultural forms. However, the superstructure also works to reinforce and maintain the base, creating a cycle that supports capitalist power.

These ideas all explore how capitalism shapes consciousness, culture, and power dynamics, and how these forces work to maintain or challenge the status quo.
