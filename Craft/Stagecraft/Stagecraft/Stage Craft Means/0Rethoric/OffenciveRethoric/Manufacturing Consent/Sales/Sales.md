### **Prospecting & Lead Generation**

- **Outbound Sales**
    
    - Actively reaching out to leads (cold calling, cold emailing, etc.).
    

- **Inbound Sales**
    
    - Leads coming to you via marketing (SEO, social media, ads, etc.).
    

- **Lead**
    
    - A potential customer.
    

- **Pipeline**
    
    - The stages a lead goes through before closing.
    

- **Prospect**
    
    - A lead that has shown interest and fits your target audience.
    
    - **Qualified Lead (SQL, MQL, PQL)**
        
        - **Marketing Qualified Lead (MQL)** – A lead that engaged with marketing efforts (e.g., signed up for a newsletter).
        
        - **Sales Qualified Lead (SQL)** – A lead deemed ready for direct sales interaction.
        
        - **Product Qualified Lead (PQL)** – A lead that has used your product in some capacity (e.g., free trial) and is likely to convert.
        
    

- **Conversion Rate** – The percentage of leads that turn into customers.

- **Objection Handling** – Addressing concerns raised by the prospect.

### **Sales Process & Closing Deals**

- **Discovery Call** – An initial conversation to assess the prospect’s needs.

- **Value Proposition** – The unique benefit your product/service offers.

- **Elevator Pitch** – A concise sales pitch delivered in under 30 seconds.

- **Post-Sale & Retention**
    
    - **Churn Rate** – The percentage of customers who stop using your service.
    
    - **Customer Lifetime Value (LTV)** – The total revenue a customer is expected to generate.
    
    - **Net Promoter Score (NPS)** – A measure of customer satisfaction based on likelihood to recommend.
    
    - **Referral Program** – Encouraging existing customers to bring in new ones.
    

- **Negotiation**
    
    - **Anchor Pricing** – Starting negotiations with a high or low price to set the reference point.
    
    - **Decoy Pricing** – Adding an option that makes another seem more attractive.
    
    - **Bundling** – Selling multiple products/services together at a discount.
    
    - **Upsell** – Encouraging a customer to buy a more expensive version of a product.
    
    - **Cross-sell** – Selling complementary products (e.g., fries with a burger).
    
    - **Loss Leader** – Selling a product at a loss to attract customers for higher-margin items.
