1. Rhetoric is how you say something, not just what you say.  
    

1. Two people can say the same thing:  
    “Work hard, don’t quit.”

1. “Discipline outlasts talent. Every damn time.”  
      
    

- Ethos
    
    – Appeal to character or credibility  
    > “Trust me, I’ve done this for 10 years.”(You believe the speaker.)
    

- Pathos
    
    – Appeal to emotion  
    > “If you give up now, you’ll regret it forever.”(You feel it.)
    

- Logos
    
    – Appeal to logic or reason  
    > “Data shows this method works 3x faster.”(It makes sense.)
