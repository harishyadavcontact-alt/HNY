- Re-Sell to People you’ve already Sold to
    
    - AIt’s harder to sell to strangers than it is to satisfied customers.Upsell extra packages to happy clients.  
        Selling strangers a 25K€ package is hard. Selling strangers a 15k€ package and then upselling another 10K€ is more likely.  
        Don’t upsell AFTER you’ve shown them the final product. Sell them and then offer an upsell just before you show them the final product, before they’ve seen the results of your work.  
        Don’t seek outside investment until you’ve exhausted all upsell opportunities from current clients.
    

- Make them. Sell to them selfeves  
    

- Nothing Happens Without a Sale
    
    ** Every result—hiring, partnership, even relationships—comes down to persuading someone. Selling is the root of all achievement.  
    

- Image Sells – Appear successful so people expect success from you

- Everybody Loves a Winner
    
    Success attracts attention, customers, and talent. Publicize your wins—show results, testimonials, and progress. Perception of success snowballs reputation.  
    

- Always Make Clients Feel Valued
    
    Ensure every client feels uniquely important. High emotional return keeps them loyal far longer than any technical advantage.  
    

- Nothing Is Too Good to Be True
    
    Confidence convinces. If you act like your offer is amazing, so will customers. Don’t be afraid to promise big (if you can deliver)—people buy certainty and excitement.  
    *
    

- Nobody Is Broke
    
    People have money for what they care about. If they’re not buying, it’s because they don’t value your offer enough. Reframe and reposition it until you find what excites them.  
    

Keep Closing

- Mystery Sales
    
    ** Offer hidden bonuses and surprises. Don’t reveal everything in advance—overdeliver unexpectedly to delight customers and create buzz. People love finding they get more than they paid for.  
    

- You have to hard close
    
    Soft closing is nice, but it takes longer and you lose a lot of time.It is better to just to just go hard and either get the business or not.  
    Out of 10 prospects: it’s better to close 5 and lose 5 in 12 hours than it is to than waste 2 months selling to 10 ‘maybes.’  
    People either want to do business with you, or they don’t. Don’t let ‘Maybe’s’ waste your time  
    

- Send people their money Back
    
    ASell your product to a client. Once they pay in call them and tell them “Listen. Things have changed and now is not best time for you to buy X. We could still do the campaign but it won’t be best for you or I. It’s better to wait Y days. So, I’m sending your money back and I’ll let you know when the time is right.”  
    Then in Y days call them back and say “Hi, the time is now perfect! And for Z€ more than previous purchase you’ll maximize your offer.”  
    Doing this can effectively double your turnover with no additional effort.
    

- Have multiple approaches to a client.

- Treat Every Inquiry as a Lead
    
    * Every social media DM, email, or question could be a sale. Always follow up, respond fast, and nurture every piece of interest.  
    

- Everything Is a Sell
    
    Every interaction—introductions, emails, even small talk—is a potential sale or networking opportunity. Treat every engagement as a chance to position value and build relationships.  
    

Sells

- Sales Is Always Urgent
    
    Close deals fast. Never delay; the longer you wait, the less likely people buy. “Now” should always be your favorite sales word.  
    

- Satisfy Immediate Needs
    
    * Humans are driven by what they want now, not in the future. Shape offers to provide instant gratification or solve urgent problems for clients—procrastination kills sales.  
    

- Exploit FOMO
    
    Fear of missing out is a powerful sales driver. Highlight what potential customers might lose if they wait—bonuses, spots, special pricing—and use deadlines relentlessly.  
    

- Promise Clients a Future – Sell outcomes, not deliverables.

- Don’t sell on Price
    
    - Amazon and Walmart compete on price, and they will beat you. So sell on brand and reputation and value.  
        You’re not going to get rich off of selling 17€ products. If people won’t buy at 17, or they will only buy at 17€, then your reputation is the problem; not your price.  
        Be recognized as an ‘expert in your field.’  
        When you sell on cheap, you have more complaints and difficult customers. Constantly increase your price by constantly improving your reputation.
    

- Sell to the Need
    
    Convince people they need whatever it is you sell.  
    Don’t sell your product, sell their need and your solution.  
    It doesn’t matter how many pages your book is or how many hours of videos your training is.  
    Coffee shops sell you on price of coffee, on the type of coffee beans, on comfortable chairs, on free wifi… They rarely sell the need.  
    You NEED coffee because you’re tired
    

- Every Purchase is an Impulse Purchase
    
    When you’re no longer needed, you’re discarded. Always be needed; not wanted.  
    When you no longer need your partners, move on. Find new partners or outsource independents  
    
    - Every Purchase Is Impulse – Scarcity + urgency close sales
    

- View Offers Through Buyers’ Eyes
    
    Review your sales process as if you’re the target customer. Remove points of confusion and friction. Make it as easy and desirable as possible for them to buy.  
    

- It’s Not What You Sell, It’s How
    
    Presentation and narrative overshadow the product itself. You can build a brand and strong results selling almost anything—if you “sell the dream” and frame it right.  
    

- Create Problems Then Solve Them
    
    Sometimes you must illustrate (or create) a problem clients didn’t recognize—then swoop in with the urgent solution. Pain unlocks wallets.  
    

- Identify Problems and Sell on Them
    
    Don’t just wait for people to notice pain—actively point it out, magnify it, and show your solution. Pain-point selling nearly always works.  
    

- Gate Keep: Tell People They Can’t Have It
    
    Create exclusivity and scarcity. When something appears out of reach or limited, desire for it increases. Use this psychology in pitches and offers to raise perceived value and close deals.  
    

- Sell the Future (Dream) Promise it
    
    When you talk about the future, you’re presuming the present. Sell the future.
    

- Play on People’s Insecurities
    
    - Show the pain, sell the cure
    
    - Everybody has vices and insecurities. Twist and use them to make money.  
        Tell them what their problem is, show them your product and promise them you’ll make things better for them.  
        Are you selling a protein shake?Don’t sell: “great taste.”Tell them: “You won’t be strong without my product? Get in better shape with my product than without it.”“You need protein shakes or your time in the gym isn’t best spent
    

- Sell the Result

- Sell Results, Not Features – Image and transformation trump specs

  

- Never Compete on Price – Compete on brand, expertise and perceived value

- Make Customers Feel Happy & Important
    
    Make every client feel like a VIP. People buy based on emotion, status, and how you make them feel—not just the logical value.
