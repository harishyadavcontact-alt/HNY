> Defence

> - Spot Bullshitters Earl
>     
>     Look for signs of incompetence or dishonesty—overpromising, lack of detail, showing off assets with no substance. Remove fakes fast to protect your business.

> Offence
> 
> - What to Do When You Make Money
>     
>     When you land a big deal or windfall, don’t spend it mindlessly. Park it safely first, then plan deployments for either protection (assets) or aggressive growth (marketing, inventory).  
>     
> 
> - Feed Yourself First
>     
>     Prioritize paying yourself as the entrepreneur before allocating profits to others. If the business can’t support its founder, it’s not sustainable. Your effort deserves compensation.  
>     
> 
> - Debt Often Isn’t Real
>     
>     ** Many debts are negotiable or flexible. Don’t panic over what you owe—negotiate, restructure, or walk away if the deal makes no sense. Debt is a tool, not a trap.  
>     
> 
> - Debt Negotiation Is a Skill
>     
>     Never settle on first demand. Negotiate debts, extend terms, or reduce amounts. Many creditors and partners will accept less if you push.  
>

> - Speed,quick urgent, immediatly fast: Blitz
>     
>     1. Do More, without loss of Quality  
>         Work and react quickly. Everything needs to be done fast; and that doesn’t mean done inexpensively or cheaply. It must be quick, inexpensively and of high quality.  
>         Professional fighters learn to punch correctly, then more quickly. Punching more quickly doesn’t mean degrading the quality of your punch. Keep the same quality of your punch, and do it more quickly.  
>         When an airplane’s engines fail, the airplane still flies forward due to forward momentum. It doesn’t stop and fall out of the sky.  
>         Don’t accept unrealistic, stupid timeframes. Everyone is slow, and being slow is deadly.  
>         The longer it takes to get a business running, the less you make in the long-term.  
>         Companies say in their job descriptions they “require” a university degree to get a trial period at a company which may or may not lead to a job. But if you’re ‘willing to offer them them to work for free for one week, and if they’re not happy with your performance not to give you the job.  
>         Having a lot of clients adds to your credibility and reputation, even if you’re ultimately losing money.

> - Start Ideas for Free
>     
>     1. You don’t need investors.  
>         You can make a simple free website with photos of products you don’t have yet but can obtain once orders come in, and you’ve a way to get paid.  
>         First get paid then figure out how to provide the product quickly afterwards. Worst case scenario give them their money back.You only lose when money isn’t coming in. Create your front-end first; worry about backend later.  
>         When a businesses cost nothing to launch, you can launch many and then invest only in those that show revenue. It costs nothing to set up a new revenue stream.
>     
>     1. – Launch first, spend later; validate with revenue

> - Never Say No to Money
>     
>     Always be able to accept payment in any form that clears. Whether it’s card, cash, crypto, or barter—don’t lose sales by being too rigid about how you’re paid.  
>     
> 
> - Use What You’ve Got
>     
>     1. Turn every personal asset or skill into cash
>     
>     1. Got a brother who can create websites? You now own a website creation company.  
>         Make a list of everything you have and how you can use it to make money.  
>         “How can I bring money in without spending any money out.” Always ask yourself “How can I increase revenue AND decrease cost?”  
>         If you’re talented and your idea is good, you don’t need investment money to launch.

> 
> - Make Business Your Existence
>     
>     ** Blur the lines between work and life. See opportunities everywhere—at dinner, at the gym, online. Business is not just a job; it’s an identity and worldview.  
>     
> 
> - Take Every Offer Seriously
>     
>     Listen to every pitch and idea—sometimes the most unexpected conversations produce your next big opportunity. Don’t let assumptions blind you to potential.  
>     
> 
> - Always Do Something
>     
>     Inaction kills momentum and insight. Always have tasks moving—outreach, testing, learning, tweaking. Action creates luck, and opportunity reveals itself to those in motion.

> Edge
> 
> - You Don’t Need to Understand Everything to Profit
>     
>     Don’t delay action waiting to know it all. Leverage people who have expertise you lack and focus on making money—not on mastering every detail yourself.  
>     These lessons stress action, leverage, vigilance, and relentless focus on personal and business growth.  
>     
> 
> - Play to Strengths, Outsource Weakness
>     
>     Double down on your best skills. Don’t waste time fixing every weakness—instead, delegate or hire out what you can’t do well. Focus energy where you multiply results.  
>     
> 
> - Leverage What You Don’t Know
>     
>     * Don’t waste time becoming an expert in everything. Pay or hire specialists for areas outside your strength; focus on your ROI.  
>     
> 
> - Rules Are Meant to Be Broken
>     
>     ** If a rule stops you from winning, find the loophole, push the boundaries, or pay to bend it. Winners adapt; losers follow all the rules and lose speed.

> - Stay Out of Party Politics Until You're Wealthy
