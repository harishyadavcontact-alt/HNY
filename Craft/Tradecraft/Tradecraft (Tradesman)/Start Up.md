- 1. Stage of Startup
    
    Incubators: Help early-stage or idea-stage startups. Sometimes, you don’t even need a product—just a concept.  
    Accelerators: Work with more mature startups—those that already have a product or MVP and want to scale quickly.  
      
      
    ---  
    2. Duration  
    Incubators: Long-term support, 6 months to a few years. Flexible timelines.  
    Accelerators: Short-term, intense programs, typically 3–6 months.  
      
      
    ---  
    3. Focus  
    Incubators: Focus on nurturing the idea, refining the business model, and helping founders build the foundation.  
    Accelerators: Focus on growth, fundraising, and scaling rapidly.  
      
      
    ---  
    4. Funding & Equity  
    Incubators: May or may not offer funding; usually no or low equity taken.  
    Accelerators: Often provide seed funding in exchange for equity (5–10%).  
      
      
    ---  
    5. Environment & Resources  
    Incubators: Often tied to universities, research centers, or local governments. Provide co-working spaces, mentorship, and basic services.  
    Accelerators: Run by VCs or corporate innovation arms, with intensive mentorship, investor access, and demo days.  
      
    Think of it like this:  
    Incubator = Startup Kindergarten (learning and developing)  
    Accelerator = Startup Bootcamp (training to scale fast)
