# Investment Approaches

**Value Investing:** A strategy where investors look for stocks trading below their intrinsic value. Value investors search for companies with strong fundamentals (good earnings, strong balance sheets, etc.) that are underprized by the market. This approach was popularized by Benjamin Graham and is famously practiced by Warren Buffett. Value investors typically hold investments for longer periods and focus on company fundamentals rather than short-term market movements.

**Hedge Funds:** Investment funds that pool capital from accredited investors (high net worth individuals or institutions) and use various strategies to generate returns. Unlike mutual funds, hedge funds can use leverage, derivatives, short-selling, and alternative strategies. They typically charge a management fee (often 2%) plus a performance fee (often 20% of profits).

**Dynamic Hedging:** A risk management strategy that involves continuously adjusting a portfolio's hedges as market conditions change. Options traders and portfolio managers use this approach to maintain a desired risk profile as prices move. It requires frequent trading and adjustment of positions.

**Fat Tail Hedge:** An investment strategy designed to protect against "fat tail" events - extreme market movements that occur more frequently than predicted by normal distribution models. These hedges aim to provide protection against market crashes and are often implemented using options, volatility instruments, or other derivatives that increase in value during market stress.

Nassim Nicholas Taleb's investment approach and capital philosophy centers around several key concepts:

**Taleb's Capital Philosophy**

Taleb is famous for his work on uncertainty, randomness, and extreme events ("Black Swans"). His investment philosophy emphasizes:

1. **Barbell Strategy** - Putting 80-90% of assets in extremely safe investments (like Treasury bills) while using the remaining 10-20% for highly speculative bets with limited downside but massive upside potential. This protects against catastrophic losses while maintaining exposure to potential windfall gains.

1. **Antifragility** - Creating portfolios that don't just withstand market stress but actually benefit from volatility and disorder. An antifragile portfolio gains from market chaos.

1. **Tail Risk Hedging** - Explicitly protecting against "fat tail" events (extreme market movements) using options and other derivatives that profit during market crashes.

1. **Empirica Capital** - Taleb founded this investment firm (now closed) that specialized in tail risk hedging strategies.

1. **Universal Investment Fund** - Taleb was associated with this fund which implemented his tail-hedging approach.

Taleb's approach rejects many conventional financial theories like Modern Portfolio Theory and the Efficient Market Hypothesis, arguing they underestimate the frequency and impact of extreme events. His investment philosophy focuses on surviving and thriving during periods of market turbulence rather than optimizing returns during normal times.
