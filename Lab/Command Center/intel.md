A Common Intelligence or Operations Picture (CIP/COP) dashboard is a real-time, consolidated display of data that provides situational awareness across an organization. It integrates various information streams into a single interface—often called a "single pane of glass"—to help decision-makers respond to incidents or manage daily operations efficiently.

Key Features of a COP Dashboard
Geospatial Visualization: Uses online maps to track live locations, evacuation zones, and incidents like fires or looting.
Real-Time Data Integration: Continuously updates with information from field sensors, social media, and internal databases.
Cross-Echelon Sharing: Enables different levels of command (from tactical units to headquarters) to see the same information simultaneously.
Customisable Views: Users can toggle layers on or off to focus on specific datasets, such as fuel levels, equipment readiness, or personnel status.

Common Applications
Emergency Response: Tracking natural disasters (e.g., Los Angeles fires) and coordinating evacuations.
Military Operations: Providing "connected warriors" with battlefield intelligence to make informed decisions without direct orders.
Business Logistics: Using analytics to monitor delivery routes and reroute vehicles based on live traffic.
Readiness Tracking: Monitoring the functional status of equipment and supplies across an entire force, such as the Army Readiness COP. 
Implementation Tools
Platforms like Operations Dashboard for ArcGIS are frequently used to build these systems because they support multi-monitor video walls in command centers as well as mobile tablets for field personnel.
