### Phronetic Rules: Principles for Prudence

- **Exposure Management Principles**:
	- Cap Downside: 
		Limit exposure to negative Black Swans. 
		"What is fragile should break early while it is still small."
	- Barbell Strategy:
		 Allocate 85-90% to ultra-safe assets, 10-15% to high-upside speculations. 
		 
		 - "Make sure that you have plenty of these small bets; avoid being blinded by the vividness of one single Black Swan."
	
	- Redundancy Over Optimization: Build buffers.
		"Redundancy is ambiguous because it seems like a waste if nothing unusual happens. Except that something unusual happens—usually."
- **Epistemic Humility Principles**:
	- Skeptical Empiricism: Demand evidence of absence, not absence of evidence. Taleb: "You need evidence of safety—not evidence of lack of safety."
	- Avoid Narrative Fallacy: Reject post-hoc explanations. Taleb: "Do not try to always find reasons for the uncertainty, to dress up your knowledge of the world with dogmas."
	- Respect Time and Convexity: Favor time-tested (Lindy effect). Taleb: "Invest in preparedness, not in prediction."
- **Heuristic Application Principles**:
	- Via Negativa: Remove fragilities first. Taleb: "The Platinum Rule: Do not do to others what you don't want them to do to you."
	- Skin in the Game: Ensure decision-makers bear consequences. Taleb: "Never trust the words of a man with no skin in the game."
	- Simplicity in Complexity: Prefer simple rules. Taleb: "Rely on the rule of thumb 'less is more'—what you remove may be more important than what you add."
	- These scale fractally: apply at personal (e.g., health), local (networks), national (policy), global (civilizational) levels.



Survival in the Fourth Quadrant is achieved not by learning "how to succeed," but by rigorously identifying "what to avoid." By building redundancy, clipping negative tails, and ruthlessly eliminating moral hazard, an institution stays in the game when the "unlikely" occurs. In a world we do not understand, the only true wisdom is the physical and structural preparation for the unknown.
