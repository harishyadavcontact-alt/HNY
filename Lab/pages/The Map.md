- Nature of Decision
	- Simple Decisions
		- M0: Binary;
			he critical differentiator is the payoff structure: M0 (zeroth moment) refers to simple binary decisions where you only care if you are right or wrong;
		- In this quadrant, decisions are **"binary,"** the primary concern is whether a statement is "true" or "false" within a certain confidence interval.
		- These are **M0 decisions** because they depend only on the "raw" probability of an event (the zeroth moment) and **not its magnitude**.
		- In a simple decision, being "very wrong" is no different from being "slightly wrong"—the only thing that matters is the frequency of occurrence
	- Complex Decisions
		- M1+ (Complex): Incorporate impact magnitude.
		- M1+: Complex Payoffs
			; M1+ (higher moments) refers to complex payoffs where the magnitude of the event dictates the result.
		- In these Decisions, You do not just care of the frequency—but of the impact as well." Decisions depend on higher moments (variance, skewness).
		- Operate by assessing exposure: leverage amplifies errors, so de-leverage in uncertainty.
		- The second type of decisions is more complex. You do not just care of the frequency—but of the impact as well, or, even more complex, some function of the impact. So there is another layer of uncertainty of impact.
		- M1+, as they depend on higher moments of the distribution). When you invest you do not care how many times you make or lose, you care about the expectation: how many times you make or lose times the amount made or lost.
- Nature of Volatility:
	- Thin Tail
		- Mediocristan (Thin-Tailed): Gaussian-like distributions; exceptions are inconsequential.
		- "Add the heaviest person on the planet to a sample of 1000. The total weight would barely change."
		- Operate with conventional statistics (means, variances); reliable for physical measurements like height.
	- Fat Tail
		- Extremistan (Thick-Tailed): Power-law distributions; rare events dominate.
		- "Add Bill Gates to your sample: the wealth will jump by a factor of >100,000." Includes "I don't know" categories for epistemic uncertainty.
		- Operate via heuristics, not models; assume fat tails in socio-economic variables.
