The turkey problem is

**a parable that illustrates the dangers of assuming that the future will be the same as the past**

. It's also known as the "turkey illusion".

**How the parable goes**

- A turkey is fed and cared for every day, increasing its confidence that it will be fed again

- On the night before Thanksgiving, the turkey is certain that it will be fed again

- However, the turkey is slaughtered the next day by the person who cared for it

**What the parable teaches**

It's dangerous to assume that the future will be the same as the past
