# Biotic

> Relating to the living world. The biotic universe is defined by one thing the abiotic lacks: striving. Every organism strives toward an objective it does not understand — lodging its lineage's genes into the future. This striving is made possible by heredity.

---

## Definition

**Biotic** = living; relating to life. The biotic universe encompasses all organisms and everything they produce — including behaviors, cultures, tools, and institutions (all built by organisms shaped by evolution).

> "If it evolved, it's fair game."
> — Heather Heying

---

## The Defining Feature: Striving

What makes the biotic world different from the abiotic is **striving** — the directed behavior of organisms toward a goal they do not consciously understand.

- A sea otter striving to crack open a clam thinks it wants the clam. It is actually striving to lodge its genes into the future.
- A salmon swimming upstream thinks it wants to spawn. It is executing a program to get its genes to the next generation.
- A bishop building a cathedral thinks he is serving God. He may be investing in lineage-level fitness at a scale he cannot perceive.

> "It is built to strive towards an objective that you do not need to know in order to accomplish it."
> — Bret Weinstein

---

## What Makes Biotic Different From Abiotic

The key ingredient is **[[Heredity]]**: the passing of information from one generation to the next. When you add heredity to abiotic selection, you get **adaptation** — the progressive accumulation of traits that help organisms persist and reproduce.

```
Selection (abiotic) + Heredity = Adaptation (biotic)
```

---

## The Hierarchy of Biotic Complexity

The biotic world has layers, each more complex and less predictable than the last:

```
Simple cells
↓
Multi-cellular organisms
↓
Social animals
↓
Humans — "brimming with paradoxes born of what makes us distinct"
```

As complexity increases, predictability decreases. Physics is near a "theory of everything." The inner workings of even the simplest living cells are not fully understood.
