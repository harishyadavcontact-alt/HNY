**Religion and**

All cultures have ritual. Death rituals are ubiquitous, birth rituals nearly so.

Some rituals are rites of passage to celebrate new babies, coming of age, marriage. There are rituals—traditions, perhaps, given their reliably repeating nature—to celebrate the first planting of the year, and the harvest, and astronomical events like the solstices and the equinoxes. As we have come to live in larger and larger groups, surrounded by ever more anonymity in our daily life, regular holidays, with their attendant shared cultural norms, help to keep us in sync, to act as though we are in fact part of something larger than ourselves.

  

Rituals, which are not inherently religious but have a strong tendency to be so, often include food, music, and dance.15
