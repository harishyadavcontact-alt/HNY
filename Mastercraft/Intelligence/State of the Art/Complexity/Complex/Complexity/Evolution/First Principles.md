**Maxim:** Start from irreducible assumptions; build reasoning upward from foundations.**Attribution:** [SOURCE]

**Central Argument:**  
First principles are assumptions that cannot be deduced from any other assumption. They are foundational like axioms in mathematics. Thinking from first principles means starting from these irreducible foundations rather than building on inherited frameworks that may contain unexamined assumptions. This is “a powerful mechanism for deducing truth.”

**Source:** [EXPLICIT] Hunter-Gatherer’s Guide to the 21st Century, Introduction - “First principles are those assumptions that cannot be deduced from any other assumption. They are foundational (like axioms, in math), and so thinking from first principles is a powerful mechanism for deducing truth, and a worthy goal if you are interested in fact over fiction.”

**Benefit:** First principles thinking “helps one avoid falling prey to the naturalistic fallacy”—deriving ought from is.

**Epistemological Status:** This is foundational methodological principle. It ensures reasoning is grounded rather than inherited.
