# Genes

> The units of heredity. The information substrate that is passed from generation to generation, selected upon, and expressed (or not) in organisms. Genes have unbridled ambition and zero vanity — they will do anything to reach the future, including nothing.

---

## Definition

Genes are the heritable units of biological information — encoded in DNA — that are passed from parents to offspring and shape the development of organisms.

A human genome contains approximately **20,000 active genes**, with perhaps 5 variants per gene (~100,000 variants total), encoding a 30 trillion cell organism with ~200 distinct cell types.

---

## The Core Property: Unbridled Ambition, Zero Vanity

Genes do not "want" to be expressed. They do not prefer to be visible in the phenotype. The only criterion is whether a strategy gets them to the future.

> "Genes have unbridled ambition. They will do literally anything to get into the future. And zero vanity. They do not care if they show up in the phenotype or not."
> — Bret Weinstein

This means:
- **Expression** is not the goal; **persistence into the future** is the goal
- **Silence** is as valid a strategy as expression — if silence increases fitness, genes silence themselves
- **Form change** is valid — if the current form is incompatible with the future, change form (dinosaurs → birds)

---

## Silence as Strategy

In every cell of your body, most of your genes are **silent**. Your eyes don't produce digestive enzymes — not because those genes aren't there, but because they have been silenced. No argument, no coercion: the digestive enzyme genes are in perfect agreement with making you a successful organism by being quiet.

Genes can be silent:
- Within a cell type (most of the genome is silent in any given cell)
- Within a tissue
- Across an entire life
- Across multiple generations

All of these are valid strategies if they get the gene to the future.

---

## Genes and Lineage

The tautological definition of fitness: **lodging your genes as far into the future as possible**. This is what every organism is "trying" to do — even though no organism understands this objective.

Because genes can be silent and still propagate, the level of selection is not always the individual. Genes may be better served by lineage-level investments ([[Notre Dame Paradox]], [[eusociality-|eusociality]]) than by individual reproduction.

---

## Genotype vs Phenotype

- **Genotype**: the genetic information an organism carries (most of it silent in any given cell)
- **Phenotype**: what is actually expressed — the body, behavior, physiology

The same genotype can produce very different phenotypes depending on which genes are expressed in which contexts. This is why genes having zero vanity matters: phenotypic expression is just one of many possible strategies.
