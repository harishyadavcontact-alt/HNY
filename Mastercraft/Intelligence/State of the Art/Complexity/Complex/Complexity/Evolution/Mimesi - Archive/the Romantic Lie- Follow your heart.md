Girard here is trying to tear down what he conceives of as the Romantic Lie. The lie goes something like this: at the bottom we are all individuals with a core of what we can call the “authentic self.” And then there are these layers of social constraints, one on top of the other, with the origins external. The way to access authenticity, so this romantic lie goes, is by “following one’s heart” with a bold breaking free from the group, a peeling away of these social layers.
