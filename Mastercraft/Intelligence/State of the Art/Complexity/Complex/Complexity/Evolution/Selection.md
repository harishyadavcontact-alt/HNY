# Selection

> A process that causes one pattern to become more common than an alternative pattern. Not inherently biotic — exists in the abiotic universe before life begins.

---

## Core Definition

Selection = the force that determines which patterns become common and which become rare. It operates on any pattern in the universe — atoms, galaxies, organisms, behaviors.

> "Selection is not the central element of biology. Selection is the force that decides what patterns are going to be common and what patterns are going to be rare. Selection exists in the universe before there's any biology."
> — Bret Weinstein, Sophia Lecture 2

---

## Selection in the Abiotic Universe

The [[4 Properties of Pattern Prevalence]] show selection operating in pure physics:
1. Tendency to come into being
2. Durability once formed
3. Similarity (recognizability as a category)
4. Tendency to accumulate limited stuff ← **proto-competition**

Gold is selected for (durable, accumulates protons). Spiral galaxies are selected for (stable, accumulate stars). This is selection without biology.

---

## What Selection Alone Cannot Do

Selection alone cannot create the biotic universe. Without heredity, nothing improves — selection filters patterns but cannot accumulate adaptive information across generations.

**Selection + [[Heredity]] = [[Adaptation]]** ← this is the threshold into the biotic universe

---

## Selection Acts on Lineages, Not Individuals

Selection is not acting on individuals — it is constantly figuring out which level of [[Lineage]] to prioritize at any given moment:
- Sometimes the individual and their offspring
- Sometimes the extended family (kin selection)
- Sometimes the nation or civilization as a lineage
- Sometimes the species lineage

This is why individual reproductive success is only a proxy for fitness — selection actually cares about the lineage, not the organism.
