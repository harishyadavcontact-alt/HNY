# Antifragile

> The state of increasing in capability when exposed to stressors or harm (Nassim Taleb, 2012). Beyond resilience — which returns to baseline after stress — antifragile systems actually improve. Evolution itself is antifragile: stressors don't just fail to destroy it, they are the engine of its improvement.

---

## Definition

**Antifragile** (coined by Nassim Taleb, 2012): systems that gain from disorder, stress, volatility, or harm — as opposed to:
- **Fragile**: breaks under stress
- **Robust/Resilient**: survives stress, returns to baseline
- **Antifragile**: benefits from stress, becomes stronger

---

## In the Evolutionary Framework

Evolution is fundamentally antifragile:
- Stressors don't just fail to eliminate populations — they are the mechanism by which adaptation occurs
- Environmental challenges drive natural selection, which improves the fit between organism and environment
- The very process that kills the weakest makes the lineage stronger

Organisms with long childhoods ([[Long Childhood,]]) are antifragile in development: stress and challenge during the plastic period can strengthen cognitive and physical capabilities. Organisms raised in over-protected environments can emerge more fragile.

---

## The Fourth Frontier Application

Heying and Weinstein invoke antifragility as a design criterion for civilization:

> "We ought to strive to create a system that is antifragile."
> — Heying & Weinstein

A civilization designed to be antifragile:
- Is not just resistant to shocks but benefits from variety and challenge
- Contains mechanisms for learning from failure
- Avoids the brittleness of single-point optimization
