# Gamete

> A mature reproductive cell that fuses with another to form a zygote. Sperm and egg are gametes. The gamete is the vector through which genetic information crosses generations — the biological mechanism of heredity.

---

## Definition

A **gamete** is a mature reproductive cell that fuses with another gamete during sexual reproduction to form a zygote, which then develops into a new organism.

In most animals:
- **Male gametes**: sperm — small, motile, produced in enormous numbers
- **Female gametes**: eggs (ova) — large, non-motile, produced in limited numbers

---

## Why It Matters for Evolution

The gamete is the unit of genetic transmission across generations. It is where the parent's genetic information is packaged and passed to the offspring. Because selection acts on the consequences of gene transmission, the gamete is the bottleneck through which evolutionary pressures flow.

The asymmetry between male and female gametes (one tiny and abundant, one large and rare) drives much of the evolution of mating systems, sexual selection, and parental investment.

---

## Gametes and the Zygotic Organism

Organisms whose developmental program is essentially "on board" at the gamete stage are called **zygotic organisms** — all programming present from the union of sperm and egg. See [[Long Childhood,]] for the contrast with plastic organisms.
