# Genotype

> The genetic constitution of an individual — the full set of genes they carry, whether expressed or not. Compare with phenotype, which is what the genotype actually produces in a given context.

---

## Definition

**Genotype**: the complete genetic information of an organism, encoded in its DNA. The genotype includes:
- All 20,000+ active genes
- Both the expressed and the silenced genes
- Variants inherited from both parents

The genotype is the underlying "code" — the potential. The [[phenotype]] is what the code produces in a specific developmental and environmental context.

---

## Genotype vs Phenotype

The distinction matters because the same genotype can produce different phenotypes:
- Same genes, different developmental environment → different expression
- Same genes, different cell type → different genes silenced/expressed (your eye cells have the same genome as your stomach cells, but different genes are active)

This is why genes having "zero vanity" ([[Genes — Unbridled Ambition Zero Vanity]]) matters: most of the genotype is silent in any given context, but still present and potentially expressible under different conditions.

---

## In Evolutionary Biology

Selection acts on **phenotypes** — what organisms actually look and behave like. But it sorts **genotypes** — the underlying information. This is why genetically identical organisms can look different in different environments, and why evolution is ultimately about changes in genotype frequencies in a population.
