# Hier (Hierarchy)

> Hierarchical organization — the arrangement of elements in levels of nested structure. Appears in lineage (fractal nesting of biological relationships), in social animals (dominance hierarchies), and in the structure of the biosphere itself.

## Hierarchy in Biology

Biological systems are organized hierarchically at multiple scales:

| Level | Example |
|---|---|
| Molecular | Gene → protein → cell machinery |
| Cellular | Cell types within an organism |
| Organismal | Organs → organ systems → organism |
| Social | Individual → family → group → population |
| Lineage | Individual → family → clan → species → clade |

Heying and Weinstein's concept of nested fractal lineages is a hierarchy: you are simultaneously embedded in multiple lineages, each of which constitutes a level at which selection can operate.

---

## Hierarchy as Predictive Structure

In science, identifying the right level of hierarchy to analyze is often the key insight. The history of evolutionary biology includes the mistake of analyzing fitness only at the individual level — when lineage-level selection explains eusociality, monument-building, and human culture far better.
