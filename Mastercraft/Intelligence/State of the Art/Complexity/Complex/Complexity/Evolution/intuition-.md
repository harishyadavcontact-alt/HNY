# Intuition

> An unconscious conclusion that can inform the conscious mind. In the evolutionary framework, intuition is not mere guessing — it is compressed pattern recognition, often encoding adaptive wisdom that predates conscious articulation.

---

## Definition

Intuition: knowledge that arrives without conscious reasoning. An unconscious conclusion — formed through pattern matching at a level below deliberate thought — that surfaces in awareness as a feeling, a hunch, or an immediate judgment.

---

## Intuition in Science

> "It is by logic that we prove, but by intuition that we discover."
> — Henri Poincaré

Poincaré's observation matters: the history of science is not primarily a history of logical deduction. It is a history of intuitive leaps — sudden recognitions that something is true or false — subsequently confirmed (or refuted) through rigorous method.

Intuition is the generation phase. Logic is the validation phase. Both are necessary.

---

## Intuition as Compressed Wisdom

In the evolutionary framework, intuition often encodes adaptive information that was "learned" not by the individual but by the lineage — ancestral wisdom compressed into automatic responses.

This is why cultural norms, gut feelings about trust, and aesthetic responses to beauty may carry more adaptive information than explicit reasoning. The reasoning is slow; the intuition is fast because it has been running for millions of years.

---

## The Danger

Intuition evolved for an ancestral environment. In [[Hyper-Novelty|hyper-novel environments]], intuitive responses may be miscalibrated — firing on stimuli that resemble ancestral threats or opportunities but aren't.

The corrective: use intuition as a signal, not an answer. When intuition fires strongly, investigate why. Do not dismiss it. Do not accept it uncritically.
