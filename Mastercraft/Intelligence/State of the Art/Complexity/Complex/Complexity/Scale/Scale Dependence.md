A property is **scale dependent** (or **not invariant under scale transformation**) when it **changes, breaks down, or reverses** as you move between scales.

**Critical insight:** What works at one scale may be neutral, ineffective, or catastrophic at another scale.

**Examples of scale dependence:**

**Biological - Hormesis:**

- Small dose of poison → Strengthens organism (adaptive response)

- Large dose of poison → Kills organism

- The "beneficial" property is scale-dependent

**Social - Collective action:**

- One person withdrawing money → Rational precaution

- Everyone withdrawing money → Bank run, systemic collapse

- Individual rationality becomes collective irrationality

**Temporal - Signal vs noise:**

- Daily stock price movements → Mostly noise, unpredictable

- Decade-long stock trends → Signal emerges, patterns visible

- What appears random at one timescale has structure at another

**Organizational - Coordination:**

- 5-person team → Direct communication works

- 10,000-person organization → Requires formal hierarchy and bureaucracy

- Communication strategies are scale-dependent
