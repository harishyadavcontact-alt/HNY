A property is **scale invariant** (or **invariant under scale transformation**) when it **maintains the same characteristics regardless of the scale** at which you observe it.

**Examples of scale invariance:**

- **Fractals:** Coastline roughness looks identical whether viewed from satellite or ground level

- **Power laws:** The relationship between earthquake magnitude and frequency follows the same pattern across all scales

- **Physical constants:** Speed of light remains constant regardless of reference frame

- **Self-similar patterns:** Branching patterns in trees, rivers, blood vessels, lightning

**Mathematical signature:** f(cx) = c^α f(x) - the function scales predictably with a consistent exponent.
