- Risk of irreversable harm
Our argument—that the real requires peril—can lead to niceties about the mind-body problem, but don’t tell your local philosopher.

Now, one may argue: once inside the machine, you may _believe_ that you have skin in the game, and experience the pains and consequences as if you were living the actual harm. But this is once _inside,_ not outside, and there is no risk of irreversible harm, things that linger and make time flow in one direction not the other. The reason a dream is not reality is that when you suddenly wake up from falling from a Chinese skyscraper, life continues, and there is no _absorbing barrier,_ the mathematical name for that irreversible state that we will discuss at length in Chapter 19, along with _ergodicity,_ the most powerful concept I know.
