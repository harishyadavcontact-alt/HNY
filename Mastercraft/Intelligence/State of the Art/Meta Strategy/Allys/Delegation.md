- Always Sharpen Your Tools
    
    Keep rehearsing your sales scripts, refining your offers, and upgrading your mindset. Stagnation is death—continual improvement is essential.  
    

- Keep Tools Sharp (Systems & People)**
    
    Continually train, update, and audit both your systems and your team. What works today may fail tomorrow if left unchecked.  
    

- Assume Work Wasn’t Done
    
    Don’t trust blindly. Always verify team members have completed tasks. Systems and double-checks are critical—trust equals audit in business.  
    

- HR Is BS
    
    Forget corporate HR. Company culture flows from the founder, not bureaucracy or paperwork. Be hands-on, directly shape standards, and keep things lean and personal.  
    

- Loyal Worker Always
    
    Loyalty in your team is invaluable. Recognize and reward dedication. Empower and promote workers who prove trustworthy and stick with you through tough periods.  
    

- Your Staff Don’t Work Hard
    
    Assume employees are less driven than you. Set high standards, measure results, and replace underperformers quickly. Mediocrity spreads if you’re not tough on expectations.  
    

- You Don’t Need an Army, You Need Special Forces
    
    A small, elite, high-output team will crush a bigger, average one. Focus on hiring, training, and retaining “A-players;” avoid swelling your team with underachievers.  
    

- Never Make Anyone Irreplaceable
    
    Cross-train your team and document every process. Ensure no one holds critical knowledge or access that could cripple your business if they leave. Always have passwords, systems, and backups in place.  
    

- Money Will Never Motivate Staff
    
    After basic needs are met, staff are motivated by purpose, respect, growth, and being part of something bigger. Build company culture and loyalty—don’t rely solely on pay raises.  
    

- Skin in the game
    
    1. Work With People Who Have Skin in the Game – Alignment beats credentials.
    

- Make sure you really Need Your Partner, Make sure your Partner and Staff really need you.
    
    A house, an insurance policy, even if it takes a lot of time, at the point of buying it is an impulse purchase.  
    This is why you must hard sell. If I can buy your product any time I want, then I’ll put it off until later, and later will probably never buy.

- Staff Must Make Money or Save Time – Fire anyone who does neither.
    
    1. Sales staff bring money in. Personal assistants can save you time.Coming to work late and leaving early… Sick days… lunch, coffee and cigarette breaks, browsing social media all cost YOU money. Inefficient staff waste a lot of your time and money.  
        Evaluate staff productivity frequently and fire staff who no longer safe you time or money. Especially if they don’t even cover their wage.  
        Staff who are doing a great job meeting targets need to work on meeting them more quickly.“It took you 2 weeks. How can you do it in 1 week?”  
        For time saved such as personal assistants, include how much money you could make instead of doing those tasks yourself.  
        There’s always more work to do, especially for startups, and finding work to do adds up. The more work a staff member gets done, the more valuable they become.  
        
    

- Reward Loyal People With More Work – Never let good talent idle

- Identify All the Ways you can do your Job Better
    
    Don’t just do your job bare minimum.  
    Make a complete list of all the things you could be doing to do a better job, then ask yourself why you don’t do them.  
    Usually the answer will be because you’re not incentivized to do so.  
    With that list, calculate the revenue you could earn if you did all that, and now negotiate with your boss.  
    “Boss, I want to do X and Y, and I calculate if I do X and Y the company could save Z€ and earn A€. Give me B weeks to prove it to you, and if I do and meet my objectives, reward me for it by giving me C. Deal?”
