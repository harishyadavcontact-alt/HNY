- In Marx’s _Economic and Philosophic Manuscripts of 1844_, he expands on the concept of the _alienation of labor_, which occurs when workers become disconnected from the products of their labor. Under capitalism, workers produce goods not for their own use but for the profit of capitalists, resulting in a loss of control over their work and their identity. This alienation manifests in four ways: from the product of labor, from the act of production, from other workers, and from the worker’s own humanity.
