> - Sell your Clients Future
>     
>     (Promise it) **When you talk about the future, you presume the present. When you tell your clients “We are going to work together for a very long time, in the future, I’ll be able to provide you with this great thing.” You are insinuating that you two are already in business together. You’ll be much more likely to sell now if you proclaim the future. (ex. On how to do this with women even in premium version) I know what’s going to happen now because I know what’s going to happen in the future. (good example in premium) Compare these two pitches: “If you buy my ebook, you’ll see why girls are texting you and what they mean by it.” Vs. “After you see my ebook, you’ll see what girls mean by what they’re texting and it’s going to go exactly as I’ve told you.” You can sell with the future on a 17 dollar ebook, a $500,000 advertising deal.

> - Re-sell to people you’ve already sold to
>     
>     Re-selling to past clients (when you did a half good job), is the easiest money you’ll ever make. At T2, we’d sell these $18k - $19k deals (at no profit), if they liked what we made, we would tack on a 5k up-sale for some “incredible airtime package that we just got somehow”. We couldn’t sell that 24k total at the start, but it was much easier to sell a 19k ad and a 5k bonus at the end than selling one 24k ad by itself. I had a friend ask to borrow money. He wanted $200,000. I asked him: “How many customers do you have?” He said: “about 10,000” Tate: “Well, there’s your money right there!” Make an email campaign, throw out a crazy discount, it doesn’t have to be for the public. Press send and pow! it’s 200 grand!! What do you need me for? These people have bought from you before, and you delivered; there’s your money! It’s sitting right there, go get it!  
>     Appeal to and serve your cohorts (long-time customers) before you seek out new customers. You will find it much easier to make money reselling to old customers rather than spending extra money on marketing in the hopes of acquiring new customers.
