**Maxim:** Major innovations begin with minority of one against consensus; consensus is not indicator of truth.  

**Central Argument:**  
Every great idea starts with a minority of one. This is not anecdotal observation but systematic pattern. Consensus is actually an unreliable or inverse indicator of truth during breakthrough moments. When new paradigms emerge, they typically emerge in opposition to existing consensus, not in alignment with it.

“Every great idea starts with a minority of one.”

**Relationship to NODE 1.1:** This is a corollary. If truth were determined by consensus, breakthroughs would not occur against consensus. Since they do, consensus cannot be truth-determining.

**Epistemological Status:** Validates NODE 1.1 through historical pattern.
