### Why You Feel Like "You" Matter Most

Evolution's constraint: When adaptation occurred, organisms had limited reach. Your ancestors could only directly affect their immediate offspring.

- **The illusion:** You think selection cares about you as an individual.

- **The reality:** What matters is the gene pool your genes circulate in. Spending every waking hour trying to increase your personal gene count three generations out wouldn't move the needle—because pool-level dynamics dominate.

**Implication:** Sometimes the most adaptive move is to prioritize larger lineages (tribe, nation, species) over personal reproduction.
