NODE 4.5: CONVERGENCE AXIOM - HISTORICAL VS. ECOLOGICAL

**Maxim:** Same trait has opposite evidential value at different temporal scales: confusing for history, informative for ecology.**Attribution:** [SOURCE]

**Central Argument:**  
Convergent evolution (independent evolution of similar traits in unrelated lineages) illustrates scale-dependent evidence. A snake and worm both lack legs. For historical relationships, this is confusing—it obscures phylogeny because it creates false appearance of similarity. For ecological relationships, this is informative—it reveals that leglessness is a convergent solution to similar ecological problems.

**Source:** [EXPLICIT] Lecture 3 (Heather Heying) - “If you have a snake and a worm and their shared lack of legs is confusing in terms of trying to figure out what their historical relationship is. The shared lack of legs is actually informative in trying to figure out what it is that they do with their lives, what their ecology is.”

**Epistemological Status:** This concrete example demonstrates NODE 4.1 and NODE 4.4. Same evidence, different evidential value at different scales.
