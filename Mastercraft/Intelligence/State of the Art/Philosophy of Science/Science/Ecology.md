**Maxim:** All organisms, including humans, have ecology; all environment is ecological.**Attribution:** [SOURCE]

**Central Argument:**  
Humans are often treated as exceptions—as having culture but not ecology. This is error. All organisms have ecology. All environment is ecological. The same ecological principles that apply to snakes apply to humans. This is not reductionism but recognition that humans participate in ecological relationships like all organisms.

**Source:** [EXPLICIT] Lecture 3 (Heather Heying) - “We often if we’re if you’re not thinking in biological terms, you don’t think of humans having an ecology. But our argument is it’s it’s all it’s everything all of our environment is ecology.”

**Epistemological Status:** This is a principle about human nature: humans are ecological beings, not exceptions to ecology. This has implications for how human behavior should be understood.
