**Maxim:** Complicated systems approach complete explanation; complex systems show increasing resistance to understanding.**Attribution:** [INFERRED from source]

**Central Argument:**  
The complexity hierarchy reveals a qualitative distinction, not merely quantitative. Complicated systems—no matter how intricate—can be understood through sufficient analysis. Complex systems—with emergent properties, feedback loops, recursive self-reference—resist reduction and complete explanation. This is not a limitation to overcome but a feature of system type.

**Source Support:** [EXPLICIT] Hunter-Gatherer’s Guide - Physics approaches “complete model.” Biology “nowhere near fully understood” and “degree of mystery compounds.” Humans produce “profound mysteries.”

**Relationship to NODE 2.1:** This is the qualitative implication of the complexity hierarchy. The shift from physics to biology is not quantitative (more complex physics) but qualitative (different type of complexity).

**Epistemological Status:** This determines what explanatory standards are realistic at different levels. Explains why complete understanding is appropriate goal in physics but not in biology or psychology.

**Note:** The framework does not state this distinction explicitly as “complicated vs. complex,” but the distinction is clearly present in the hierarchy’s structure.
