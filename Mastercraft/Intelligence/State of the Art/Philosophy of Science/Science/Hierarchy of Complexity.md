**Maxim:** Reality is hierarchically structured by complexity; predictability decreases with increasing complexity.**A

**Central Argument:**  
Reality itself—not our understanding of reality, but reality itself—is structured according to hierarchy of complexity. This hierarchy determines what can be known and predicted. As one moves up the hierarchy, predictability decreases systematically. This is not limitation of current knowledge but feature of reality’s structure.

**Source:** [EXPLICIT] Hunter-Gatherer’s Guide to the 21st Century, Introduction - “In physics we seem tantalizingly near a ‘theory of everything,’ which really means a complete model of the least complex, most fundamental layer of explanation. As we move up in complexity, things become less and less predictable.”

**Hierarchy Structure:** [EXPLICIT]  
- Physics: least complex, most fundamental  
- Moving up in complexity: things become less predictable  
- Biology: complexity increases, mystery compounds  
- Animals: neurological feedback introduces recursive complexity, unpredictability “jumps”  
- Humans: “brimming over with profound mysteries”

**Source for Full Hierarchy:** [EXPLICIT] Hunter-Gatherer’s Guide to the 21st Century, Introduction -

“Near the top of the stack we reach biology, where processes inside even the simplest living cells are nowhere near fully understood. Things only get more complex from there. As cells begin to function in coordinated ways, becoming organisms made up of distinct tissues, the degree of mystery compounds.

The unpredictability jumps again in animals, governed by sophisticated neurological feedbacks that themselves investigate and predict the world, and once again as animals become social and begin to pool their understanding and divide their labor. Nowhere are we more regularly stumped than we are in understanding ourselves.”

**Epistemological Status:** This is foundational to understanding what types of questions are answerable and how. It explains why physics can approach complete explanation but biology cannot.

**Critical Note:** The framework emphasizes this is about reality structure, not about current human knowledge gaps. It is not saying “we haven’t figured out biology yet” but “biology is fundamentally different in kind from physics.”
