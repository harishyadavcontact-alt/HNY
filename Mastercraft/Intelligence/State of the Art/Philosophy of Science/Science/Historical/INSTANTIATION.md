UNIVERSAL-PARTICULAR INTEGRATION -
**Maxim:** Universals are instantiated in particular contexts; every instance is contextual even when principle is universal.**Attribution:** [INFERRED from source]

**Central Argument:**  
While universals are discovered and identical everywhere, their instantiation in particular contexts is always particular. The universal (Fibonacci mathematics) appears in particular forms (spiral staircases, seashells) that are products of evolution and history. The universal principle is independent of instantiation. Every instantiation occurs within some particular context.

**Source Support:** [EXPLICIT] Lecture 1 (Heather Heying) - Fibonacci series appears “represented in this shell but not by this shell”—the shell is particular instantiation of universal principle.

**Epistemological Status:** This explains how universals and particulars relate: universals are principles, particulars are manifestations. One must understand both to understand any phenomenon.

**Note:** This distinction is present in sources but stated implicitly through example rather than explicitly as principle.
