**Maxim:** Direct observation and reasoning from first principles precede authority consultation.**Attribution:** [SOURCE]

**Central Argument:**  
When encountering a phenomenon, observe directly first. Use your senses and reasoning to form initial understanding. Then consult authorities. Compare your understanding with theirs. This hierarchy—observation, reasoning, authority consultation—develops independent judgment. This does not mean authorities are untrustworthy but that direct engagement should come first.

**Source:** [EXPLICIT] Lecture 1 (Heather Heying) - “Use what you have in your head and what you can see with your senses to try to understand before you refer to authorities, no matter how good you think they might be.”

**Epistemological Status:** This operationalizes epistemic humility and independence. It prevents intellectual passivity.

**Implication:** This grounds understanding in observable phenomena rather than accepting mediated descriptions uncritically.
