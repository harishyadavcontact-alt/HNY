**Maxim:** Evidence appropriateness is radically dependent on the timescale of the question.**Attribution:** [SOURCE]

**Central Argument:**  
What counts as evidence depends on timescale. The same phenomenon is simultaneously noise for one timescale and signal for another. This is not confusion or error but a logical consequence of how evidence relates to time. Depending on what timescale is of interest, the things that are most interesting and most aggravating are actually opposite.

**Source:** [EXPLICIT] Lecture 3 (Heather Heying) - “Depending on what time scale you’re interested in, the things that are most interesting and most aggravating are actually the opposite.”

**Epistemological Status:** This is a fundamental principle about the relationship between evidence and timescale. It explains why different subdisciplines prioritize different evidence types without either being wrong.
