**Maxim:** What is noise for one timescale is signal for another timescale.**Attribution:** [SOURCE]

**Central Argument:**  
Behavioral data is “a pain” for macroevolutionary biologists because it is noise—introduces confusion into historical inference. The same behavioral data is “the most interesting of all” for microevolutionary ecologists because it is signal—reveals function. This is not contradiction but contextual reality: signal and noise are defined by the question being asked.

**Source:** [EXPLICIT] Lecture 3 (Heather Heying) - “Behavioral characters are a pain for those macroevolutionary biologists who are trying to infer history. And there’s often a lot of noise… whereas for those of us… more interested in the smaller time scales… it’s exactly those noisy things, the behaviors, the things that change really quickly that are the most interesting of all.”

**Epistemological Status:** This is a fundamental principle: signal/noise is not intrinsic to evidence but relative to the question.
