  

- Locke's project to build a passionless world of commerce stands in stark contrast to the radical philosophy of Carl Schmitt, for whom passion, conflict, and enmity are the very essence of politics.

- For Schimitt, conflict is not a problem to be solved but a permanent feature of the human condition.

- The Core Idea: The Permanence of the Political
    
    - Schimitt's central concept is the distinction between friend and enemy.
    
    - Politics, in its purest form, is the arena where this distinction is made. It is not about debate or administration but about existential struggle.
    

- On Human Nature: Divided by Fundamental Questions, and take sides
    
    - Schmitt argues that it is an inescapable part of the human condition to be divided by fundamental questions and to be forced to take sides.
    

Peak politics = overt enemy, kill or get killed, what to kill & what to die for

- **"The high points of politics... are the moments in which the enemy is, in concrete clarity, recognized as the enemy".**

- **The enemy is not merely a competitor; they are an existential threat whose very presence forces us to confront fundamental questions about who we are.**
