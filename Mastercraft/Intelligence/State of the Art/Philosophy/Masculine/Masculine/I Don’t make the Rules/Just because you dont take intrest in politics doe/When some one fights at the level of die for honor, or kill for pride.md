The crucial takeaway from Schmitt's theory is that neutrality is an illusion. Refusing to recognize an enemy is not a passive act; it is a political choice. As Schmitt puts it, "if a part of the population declares that it no longer recognizes enemies, then... it joins their side and aids them." There is no safety in "unilateral disarmament," because an enemy that continues to fight will ultimately win against a society that values earthly life above all else.
