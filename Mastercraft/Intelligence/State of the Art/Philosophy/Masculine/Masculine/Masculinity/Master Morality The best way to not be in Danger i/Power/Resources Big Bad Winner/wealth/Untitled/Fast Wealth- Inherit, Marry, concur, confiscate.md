> Most great fortunes combine both sides—e.g., a startup founder (Getting Rich) may later use lobbying (Taking Wealth) to maintain dominance.

- **Creating Wealth: Opportunistic**
    
    - **Entrepreneurship** – Building a business that provides value.
    
    - **Innovation & Invention** – Developing new technologies, patents, or intellectual property.
    
    - **Prospecting** – Finding untapped resources (oil, minerals, startups, new markets).
    
    - **Speculation (Calculated Risk-Taking)** – Making high-risk, high-reward investments (startups, crypto, options trading).
    
    - **Investment (Value Investing)** – Allocating capital to productive assets (stocks, real estate, businesses).
    
    - **Labor & Skill Development** – Earning through expertise, skills, and high-value services.
    
    - **Brand & Influence** – Leveraging personal brand or media presence to generate income.
    
    - **Licensing & Royalties** – Earning from intellectual property (books, music, patents, franchising).
    

- **Taking Wealth: Explotative**
    
    - **Inheritance** – Receiving wealth from family.
    
    - **Marriage** – Gaining wealth through a partner.
    
    - **Chance (Lottery, Gambling, Windfalls)** – Acquiring money through luck rather than effort.
    
    - **Theft & Fraud** – Illegally or unethically acquiring money from others.
    
    - **Extortion & Blackmail** – Forcing others to give wealth under threat.
    
    - **Monopoly & Rent-Seeking** – Controlling a market and extracting wealth through pricing power.
    
    - **Graft & Corruption** – Exploiting public or corporate positions for financial gain.
    
    - **Lobbying & Regulatory Capture** – Influencing laws for financial advantage.
    
    - **Counterfeiting** – Creating fake assets or currency to extract real value.
