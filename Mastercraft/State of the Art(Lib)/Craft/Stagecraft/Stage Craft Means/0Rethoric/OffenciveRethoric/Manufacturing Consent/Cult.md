- Cult Of personality
    
    A **cult of personality**, or a **cult of the leader**,[[1]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cult_of_personality\#cite_note-mudde-1) is the result of an effort which is made to create an idealized and heroic image of a leader by a government, often through unquestioning [flattery](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Flattery) and [praise](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Praise). Historically, it has developed through techniques of mass media, [propaganda](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Propaganda), [spectacle](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Spectacle), [the arts](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/The_arts), patriotism, and [government-organized demonstrations](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Government-organized_demonstration) and rallies. A [cult](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cult) of personality is similar to [apotheosis](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Apotheosis), except that it is established by modern [social engineering techniques](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Social_engineering_\(political_science\)), usually by the state or the party in [one-party states](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/One-party_state) and [dominant-party states](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dominant-party_state). Cults of personality often accompany the leaders of [totalitarian](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Totalitarian) or [authoritarian](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Authoritarianism) governments. They can also be seen in some [monarchies](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Monarchy), [theocracies](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Theocracy), [failed democracies](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Failed_state) and even in [liberal democracies](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Liberal_democracies).

- Cult Of personality
    
    A **cult of personality**, or a **cult of the leader**,[[1]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cult_of_personality\#cite_note-mudde-1) is the result of an effort which is made to create an idealized and heroic image of a leader by a government, often through unquestioning [flattery](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Flattery) and [praise](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Praise). Historically, it has developed through techniques of mass media, [propaganda](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Propaganda), [spectacle](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Spectacle), [the arts](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/The_arts), patriotism, and [government-organized demonstrations](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Government-organized_demonstration) and rallies. A [cult](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cult) of personality is similar to [apotheosis](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Apotheosis), except that it is established by modern [social engineering techniques](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Social_engineering_\(political_science\)), usually by the state or the party in [one-party states](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/One-party_state) and [dominant-party states](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dominant-party_state). Cults of personality often accompany the leaders of [totalitarian](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Totalitarian) or [authoritarian](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Authoritarianism) governments. They can also be seen in some [monarchies](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Monarchy), [theocracies](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Theocracy), [failed democracies](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Failed_state) and even in [liberal democracies](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Liberal_democracies).
