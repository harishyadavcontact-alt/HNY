# Snake oil

1. **False Claims**
    
    1. **:** Making exaggerated or false claims about the product’s benefits without scientific evidence to support them.
    

1. **Pseudoscience**
    
    1. **:** Using scientific-sounding language or references to give the impression that the product is backed by scientific research, even if it isn’t.
    

1. **Anecdotal Evidence**
    
    1. **:** Relying on personal stories or testimonials rather than objective data to support the product’s effectiveness.
    

1. **Fearmongering**
    
    1. **:** Playing on people’s fears or insecurities to convince them that they need the product to avoid negative outcomes.
    

1. **Limited Time Offers**
    
    1. **:** Creating a sense of urgency by offering the product at a discounted price for a limited time, encouraging impulsive buying.
    

1. **Celebrity Endorsements**
    
    1. **:** Using endorsements from celebrities or influencers to lend credibility to the product, even if they have no expertise in the field.
    

1. **Hidden Costs**
    
    1. **:** Failing to disclose all the costs associated with the product, such as shipping fees or subscription charges.
    

1. **Pressure Tactics**
    
    1. **:** Using high-pressure sales tactics to push people into making a purchase before they have had a chance to fully consider the product.
    

1. **Unsubstantiated Guarantees**
    
    1. **:** Offering a money-back guarantee or promise of results without evidence to back up the claims.
    

1. **Complexity**
    
    1. **:** Making the product seem more complex or sophisticated than it really is to impress potential buyers.
