- _Structural Marxism_ is a variant of Marxist theory that emphasizes the importance of underlying structures (economic, social, and ideological) in shaping human behavior and historical outcomes. Althusser, a key figure in structural Marxism, argued that individuals are shaped by these structures, particularly through _ideological state apparatuses_ like education, religion, and the media. This approach shifts away from focusing on individual agency and class struggle as the primary drivers of history.
