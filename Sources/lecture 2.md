Source guide

This source presents a sweeping critique of modern scientific practices, arguing that the academic world is caught in a "cryptic dark age" because it has forgotten the proper **scientific method**, overly relying on consensus and data volume rather than rigorous hypothesis and **predictive power over the long term**. The speaker encourages treating **paradox** as a vital signal for potential discovery, emphasizing that the entire evolutionary process can be simplified to the tautological drive of **lodging one's genes as far into the future as possible**. This deeper understanding of **biotic dynamics** necessitates moving beyond individual reproductive success to consider the fractal organization of life, best described by the concept of **lineage**. Ultimately, the lecture seeks to provide a necessary evolutionary toolkit for addressing the profound challenges of **hyper novelty** and understanding paradoxical human cultural achievements.
