Yes — finance is a subfield of economics, but it has grown big and specialized enough that it's now often treated as its own discipline.  
  
---  
Here’s the Relationship:  
Economics = the big picture  
> Studies how people, businesses, and governments allocate scarce resources.It’s divided into:  
  
  
Microeconomics: Individuals & firms.  
Macroeconomics: Nations, inflation, interest rates, GDP.  
  
Finance = the money engine inside that big picture  
> Focuses on how money is raised, invested, managed, and transferred.It splits into:  
  
  
Personal Finance – how individuals manage wealth.  
Corporate Finance – how companies handle funding, capital, risk.  
Public Finance – how governments deal with taxes, debt, spending.  
Investment Finance – how markets work (stocks, bonds, risk, return).  
  
  
---  
Metaphor time?  
Economics is the map. Finance is the vehicle.  
Economics explains why a crash happened. Finance decides how to survive or profit from it.  
  
  
---  
So:  
> Learn economics to understand the world.Master finance to navigate it — and profit.
